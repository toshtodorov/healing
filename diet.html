Feeding Windows

When to Eat

Timing is as important as content. The fasting window on aerobic days is load-bearing for autophagy and AMPK activation.

Training Days — eat early
First mealImmediately after training (~6:30–7:00am). Protein-forward. The post-training window is when protein synthesis is highest — do not delay.
Final mealBy 6:00pm. Nothing after 6:00pm. Late meals fragment sleep architecture and suppress melatonin.
Eating window~12 hours (7am–7pm). Not a strict fast but the evening cutoff is firm.
Aerobic / Rest Days — eat later
First meal10:00am or later. The morning fasting period enables AMPK activation and autophagy — cellular clearing between neuroplasticity events. Do not eat first thing on these days.
Final mealBy 6:00pm. Same evening cutoff.
Eating window~8 hours (10am–6pm). The fasting window from previous evening's final meal to 10am = 16+ hours.
CC scheduling noteFirst meal after returning from waterfall (~3:00–3:30pm) fits naturally inside the aerobic day window. The CC session begins ~2h post-exercise regardless of meal timing.
Why the evening cutoff matters specifically here: CC and EMDR sessions produce new associations and memory reprocessing that consolidate during deep sleep. Late meals delay sleep onset, compress deep sleep, and raise overnight cortisol. Every structural change the protocol produces during the day is either cemented or partially dissolved overnight — the evening cutoff protects the work.
Priority Foods

What to Prioritize and Why

Each food has a specific mechanistic reason to be here. Not all are equally critical — bold border = highest priority for this protocol.

Neuroplasticity & BDNF

Blueberries & Blackberries
Daily — fresh or frozen, any amount
Anthocyanins + BDNF upregulation. One of the best-evidenced food-BDNF relationships. Add to every smoothie, oatmeal, or eat as-is.
Dark Chocolate 85%+
Daily — 20–40g after a meal
Theobromine + flavanols. Improves cerebral blood flow and BDNF. The 85%+ threshold matters — lower cacao = more sugar = less effect.
Walnuts
Daily — small handful
ALA (plant omega-3 precursor), polyphenols. The only nut with significant omega-3 content.
Ground Flaxseed
Daily — 1–2 tbsp in smoothie or oats
Highest plant ALA source. Lignans (anti-inflammatory). Must be ground — whole seeds pass through undigested.

Spermidine & Cellular Renewal

Wheat Germ
1–2 tbsp daily — add to smoothies or oats
Highest spermidine concentration per gram of any food. Spermidine triggers autophagy — cellular clearing between plasticity events. This is particularly important during active neuroplasticity phases.
Tempeh
Regularly — 2–3× per week, any preparation
Top concentrated spermidine source + complete protein + probiotics. Fermented so more digestible than unfermented soy. More bioavailable spermidine than most plant sources.
Mushrooms
Daily — any variety, any preparation
Ergothioneine — the compound most strongly linked to reduced disease risk in large-scale blood analysis. Heat-stable so cooking doesn't destroy it. Oyster, porcini, shiitake highest, but any variety contributes.
Strawberries
Weekly — 200–300g
Fisetin — a senolytic (clears senescent cells). Particularly relevant during active neuroplasticity phases when cellular renewal matters most.

Anti-inflammatory & Gut

Leafy Greens
Daily — large portions, raw or cooked
Nitrates (vasodilation, cerebral blood flow), folate, lutein. The volume should be substantial — not a side dish but a foundation. Spinach, kale, chard, rocket, anything dark and leafy.
Brassicas
Regularly — chop and wait 40 min before cooking
Sulforaphane (potent anti-inflammatory, crosses blood-brain barrier). Critical preparation note: chop and wait 40 minutes before cooking — this allows myrosinase enzyme to produce sulforaphane. Add mustard powder if cooking immediately.
Fermented Foods
Daily — kimchi, sauerkraut, miso, kefir
Microbiome diversity directly affects gut-brain axis signaling. L. reuteri supplement helps but food-derived diversity provides what supplements cannot.
Capers
Daily — 1 tbsp on/in anything
Highest quercetin food source per gram (~20mg/tbsp). Quercetin has senolytic properties and reduces neuroinflammation. The easiest high-value food add — put them on everything.

Blood Sugar & Metabolic

Legumes
Daily or near-daily — black beans, lentils, chickpeas
Protein + resistant starch (feeds beneficial gut bacteria). The resistant starch is only present when cooked and cooled — cold bean salads are more beneficial than hot.
Apple Cider Vinegar
1 tbsp before meals — in water or on salad
AMPK activation, blood sugar management. Takes the edge off post-meal glucose spikes. Particularly useful on training days after the protein smoothie.
Oily fish (optional): Sardines, mackerel, salmon 2–3× per week if included. ~1500–2000mg EPA+DHA per 100g serving. On fish days, skip krill and omega-3 top-up supplements — the food provides the dose. Not required if krill + algae supplementation is consistent.
Practical Patterns

Meal Patterns for the RB Context

Community kitchen, shared meals, limited personal cooking windows, high competence when cooking is available.

Training Day — Post-Workout Smoothie (6:30am)

  • Plant protein base: 30–40g protein (hemp, pea, or brown rice protein powder)
  • Blueberries or blackberries: 150–200g frozen
  • Wheat germ: 2 tbsp
  • Ground flaxseed: 2 tbsp
  • Walnuts: small handful
  • Optional: banana for texture, cacao powder for flavanols
  • Liquid: oat milk, coconut water, or water

This smoothie covers: protein, BDNF foods (berries), spermidine (wheat germ), omega-3 (flax + walnuts), and antioxidants — all in under 3 minutes prep.

Training Day — Main Meal (midday, with the community)

  • Load the plate with greens first, then build on top
  • Add legumes (black beans, lentils, chickpeas) as the primary protein source if available
  • Add fermented food if available (kimchi, sauerkraut) — even a small amount counts
  • Capers: add 1 tbsp to whatever is on the plate
  • Dark chocolate: 20–30g as dessert or immediately after
  • ACV: 1 tsp in water before the meal if practical

Strategy at community meals: treat the shared food as a base and add personal priority ingredients (capers, wheat germ in a bag, dark chocolate) to whatever is served.

Aerobic Day — First Meal (10am+, after the fast)

  • Break the fast with something substantial and protein-forward
  • Option A: Large grain bowl — cooked quinoa or brown rice, roasted vegetables, brassicas, legumes, tahini dressing, capers
  • Option B: Tempeh stir-fry with leafy greens, mushrooms, and fermented side
  • Option C: Smoothie bowl — protein smoothie base with toppings (wheat germ, berries, walnuts, cacao)
  • Brassica prep note: if cooking, chop 40 min earlier. If time-constrained, add mustard powder to cooked brassicas to restore sulforaphane.

Limited Kitchen Access — Quick Assembly Strategy

  • Always on hand: Wheat germ (shelf-stable), capers, ground flaxseed, walnuts, dark chocolate 85%+, frozen berries
  • These add to anything: Add wheat germ and flaxseed to any meal. Add capers to any savory dish. Dark chocolate after any meal. These require no cooking.
  • Batch cooking when kitchen is available: Large batches of legumes (freeze in portions), roasted brassicas (reheat quickly), tempeh (marinated and pan-fried, stores well)
  • At community meals: Eat what is served, add your priority items, focus on volume of greens and legumes
Daily Drinks

Matcha & Green Tea Protocol

Matcha — Morning
WhenMorning with or after first meal. Not on an empty stomach if it produces nausea.
What it provides300–400mg L-theanine (more than supplement capsules), EGCG (anti-inflammatory, neuroprotective), and moderate caffeine paired with L-theanine for calm focused alertness without the cortisol spike of coffee.
CC day noteMorning matcha means L-theanine is already present at baseline — 300–400mg per cup, more than any supplement capsule provides.
Iron absorptionSpace matcha at least 1 hour from iron-rich meals or iron supplements. EGCG inhibits non-heme iron absorption. Not a reason to avoid it — just observe the spacing.
Green Tea — Afternoon
Which teaKukicha (twig tea) or sencha. Lower caffeine than matcha. Safe 2–3 hours before sleep. Kukicha is particularly low-caffeine and has a pleasant roasted quality.
WhenAfternoon, 2–3 hours before sleep target. Replaces any temptation toward late caffeine while providing EGCG and trace L-theanine.
Iron absorptionSame rule as matcha — space 1 hour from iron-rich meals.
Supplement–Food Interactions

Timing Rules

These are the timing rules that actually affect absorption or efficacy.

Critical timing rules
NMN 500mg sublingualEmpty stomach or before other morning food. Dissolves under tongue 1–2 min. Takes it before eating to maximize NAD+ precursor absorption.
D3/K2With fat-containing food. D3 is fat-soluble — absorption is significantly impaired without fat. Take with the morning meal, not first thing on an empty stomach.
Phosphatidylserine 300mgAfternoon with meal. Not in the evening — can occasionally interfere with sleep.
B-complexMorning only. B vitamins can increase alertness — late dosing disrupts sleep.
Magnesium (both forms)Bedtime only. If also supplementing zinc, space 2+ hours from magnesium — they compete for absorption.
Matcha / green teaSpace 1 hour from iron-rich meals. EGCG inhibits non-heme iron absorption — relevant if diet is high in plant-based iron (lentils, spinach, tempeh).
Krill oil + algae oilWith food. Not required to be with fat specifically (the oils are their own fat), but taken with a meal improves tolerability.
Matcha (L-theanine source)Morning only. Provides 300–400mg L-theanine + EGCG. Space 1 hour from iron-rich foods — EGCG inhibits non-heme iron absorption.
Resveratrol 500mg (Phase 5+)Weekdays only, with fat-containing food. Fat significantly increases resveratrol bioavailability. Synergistic with NMN via sirtuin pathway.
Berberine 500mg (Phase 5+)Not on resistance training days. Berberine activates AMPK, which can interfere with the mTOR-driven muscle protein synthesis that follows training. Take with meals on non-training days.
Krill + algae on fish daysSkip both when eating oily fish (sardines, mackerel, salmon). The food provides the dose — supplementing on top creates excess.
NAC + MDMAStop NAC 3 days before any MDMA session. Resume 48 hours after. NAC may blunt therapeutic effect via glutamate pathway interaction.
5-HTP + psilocybinNo 5-HTP or tryptophan in 24 hours before any macrodose session. Serotonin precursor loading before 5-HT2A agonism creates unpredictable interaction.
