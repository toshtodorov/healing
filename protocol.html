0B. What This Protocol Is

This is a personal healing and wellness protocol built for one specific person, addressing one specific problem: misophonia. It is the product of an extended inquiry into what neuroscience, behavioral research, plant medicine, and evidence-based nutrition each have to offer a condition for which conventional medicine has no established treatment.

The protocol integrates multiple domains — neuroplasticity science, psychedelic-assisted therapy, behavioral conditioning, nutrition, exercise physiology, and somatic work — not because complexity is a virtue, but because misophonia is a multi-layered condition that genuinely requires tools working at different levels simultaneously. Each element has a specific mechanistic reason to be here. Each earns its place.

The primary goal is clear: durable, lasting reduction in misophonic reactivity — sufficient to no longer meaningfully impair daily life, relationships, or the ability to share space with others. The wellness work woven throughout — the nutrition, the sleep architecture, the cellular health — is not a separate agenda. It builds and sustains the same biological substrate that neuroplasticity runs on. The brain that rewires most effectively is a brain that is well-nourished, well-rested, and operating from a calm physiological baseline. These things are inseparable.

Success is measured by the A-MISO-S questionnaire, SUDs trajectory, and — most importantly — behavioral change in the avoidance inventory: what situations are you no longer avoiding? The wellness dimension is tracked through HRV trajectory, bloodwork panels, and subjective vitality over time.

This protocol is a living document. It establishes a starting point and a structure — not a rigid prescription. As the work progresses, individual elements will be adjusted based on felt sense, observed effects, bloodwork, and what the nervous system is actually responding to. Some things will work better than expected and deserve more emphasis. Some will need modification. Some may be deprioritized entirely. That is not protocol failure — it is protocol intelligence. The structure here is the best current map. The territory, as always, takes precedence.

0C. How Misophonia Works and Where This Protocol Intervenes

The Core Circuit

When the misophonic brain hears a trigger sound, it involuntarily activates the sensorimotor cortex corresponding to the body part producing that sound — a mirror neuron-like process where the brain simulates the action. That motor simulation cascades into the anterior insula (threat signal), the anterior cingulate cortex (amplification), and the autonomic nervous system (fight-or-flight response). This fires faster than conscious regulation can intercept it.

When the trigger is associated with a specific person, a second layer activates simultaneously: social threat coding — the sense that the triggering person is doing something to you. And running ahead of everything is anticipatory dread: the system pre-activates before any sound occurs, keeping the cascade perpetually primed.

Three layers. Three different neurological substrates. Three different sets of tools.

Three Trigger Layers — Three Sets of Tools

Layer 1 — Acoustic-Sensory

The raw conditioned response to the sound itself.
Primary tools: counterconditioning, psilocybin, tVNS, SSP.

Layer 2 — Interpersonal-Violation

Social threat coding attached to specific people.
Primary tools: MDMA-assisted processing, EMDR, IFS.

Layer 3 — Anticipatory-Dread

Pre-exposure anxiety that keeps the system chronically pre-activated.
Primary tools: SGB, HRV biofeedback, Yoga Nidra, somatic release, tVNS, kava.

The anticipatory layer is addressed first. Without reducing chronic pre-activation, the acoustic layer cannot be worked effectively, and the interpersonal layer cannot be safely approached at depth.

Why Standard Behavioral Therapy Has a Ceiling

The misophonic threat response fires because of a conditioned circuit that activates faster than top-down regulation can reach it. Passive exposure to that cascade does not correct it — in some cases it deepens it. Counterconditioning is the operative mechanism: actively pairing the trigger with a genuinely felt positive emotional state, building a competing association strong enough to inhibit the threat response. This requires a calm nervous system, genuine felt positive emotion, sufficient neuroplasticity for the new association to write durably, and sleep to consolidate it overnight. This is what the protocol is designed to produce — those four conditions, simultaneously, repeatedly, over a year.

0D. The Four Pillars and Governing Principles

Pillar 1 — Plasticity Opening

Destabilizes the rigid misophonic circuit. Makes synapses malleable. Creates conditions in which new learning writes more deeply.
Psilocybin, MDMA, Stellate Ganglion Block, microdosing.

Pillar 2 — Behavioral Rewriting

Writes new competing associations while the circuit is open. This is the actual rewiring. Without this pillar, an open window closes on empty.
Counterconditioning (six techniques), EMDR, propranolol-assisted reconsolidation, IFS, ACT, cognitive reappraisal.

Pillar 3 — Consolidation

Cements structural changes during sleep. Sustains the vagal tone and prefrontal strength that makes rewiring hold.
tVNS, tDCS, HRV biofeedback (HeartMath), Safe and Sound Protocol, Yoga Nidra, sleep architecture.

Pillar 4 — Substrate

Sustains the biological conditions neuroplasticity runs on: BDNF, NAD+, cellular repair, reduced baseline arousal.
Exercise, fasting, nutrition, supplementation, somatic release, sleep hygiene, kava.

Governing Principles

  • The autonomic baseline determines the ceiling of all other work. Until the floor is lowered, every intervention is limited by it. This is why structural autonomic intervention comes first.
  • Counterconditioning is the primary rewriting mechanism. But treatment here means two things simultaneously: writing new associations into the circuit, and stopping daily life from continuously re-stamping the old ones. Both are required. An open plasticity window that closes on an unrewritten circuit achieves little. A circuit being rewritten while the environment keeps reinforcing the original pattern gains less than it should. The autonomic floor, the behavioral work, and the environmental containment are a single integrated effort.
  • The substrate determines how much sticks. Sleep consolidates every structural change. Neglect the substrate and gains erode between sessions.
  • The protocol must never replicate the condition it is treating. If the protocol ever starts to feel like a burden, that is a signal to reduce intensity — not push through. See Section 3.
  • Flexibility is built in, not bolted on. The structure serves the nervous system. Not the other way around.

Section 3

Daily Operating Modes

The mode determines what the day looks like — not willpower.

🟢 Green — Resourced

Indicators: Slept reasonably, body available, no rising dread, CC feels approachable, resting tension below 4/10.

Run the full current-phase rhythm. Active rewiring available and pursued. If a plasticity window is open (post-macrodose, post-SGB) — bring full effort.

🟡 Yellow — Frayed

Indicators: Poor sleep, rising irritability, tension above 5/10, dread around CC.

Support day only. Full baseline + support layer. No CC. No hierarchy work. No deliberate trigger exposure. One Yellow day requires no adjustment to the overall phase.

🔴 Red — Tactical Brake

Activate if 3 or more are present, or any single one is severe:

  • Sleep poor 3+ consecutive nights despite sleep protocol
  • Baseline reactivity rising week over week
  • CC sessions consistently ending higher than they began
  • Growing dread or avoidance around CC itself
  • Rumination increasing rather than decreasing
  • Resentment toward the protocol has appeared
Full Tactical Brake protocol

Duration: Minimum 48 hours. Extend until the system settles.

Suspend entirely: No CC, no hierarchy work, no deliberate trigger exposure, no macrodosing or MDMA, no propranolol, no EMDR.

Double the support layer: Yoga Nidra nightly (40+ min), somatic release every morning, Qi-Gong daily, HRV biofeedback daily. Exercise only if genuinely restorative.

Maintain baseline: All supplements, all sleep protections. No changes to nutrition or fasting. No protocol planning or analysis.

Return when: Sleep stable 2+ nights, tension and dread clearly lower. First session back: 20 minutes, SUDs 1–3. Confirm the system accepts the work before returning to full intensity.

If the Brake is needed more than once in a phase, revisit the phase gate. Slow the overall pace. Pushing through warning signs produces sensitization — and sensitization undoes previous gains.

Stellate Ganglion Block

Dual Sympathetic Reset (DSR)

Right stellate ganglion first → left 24–72 hours later. Ultrasound guidance non-negotiable.

What it does: Lowers the sympathetic floor through temporary interruption of overactivated NGF signaling. Not sedation — a durable reset persisting months to years. Effect develops over 1–4 weeks.

Horner's syndrome (temporary eyelid droop, pupil constriction, mild flushing) is expected and confirms effectiveness. Resolves within hours.

Pre-SGB supplement and substance protocol

Pause 1 week before: Krill oil, omega-3 top-up (fish oil or algae oil), NAC, vitamin E, resveratrol (Phase 5 only). Resume 48 hours after.

Pause 7 days before: Microdosing.

Space at least 72 hours between any macrodose session and SGB. An active neuroplasticity window is not a contraindication — the protocol deliberately exploits this overlap.

Continue normally: Magnesium, Lion's Mane, Bacopa, Creatine, NMN, B-complex, B12, D3/K2, Ashwagandha, Lithium Orotate, Phosphatidylserine, L. reuteri.

Stop alcohol 7 days before. Prepare post-SGB CC plan before the procedure.

Phase 1 · 4F

HeartMath HRV Biofeedback

Begins Month 2. Device: HeartMath Inner Balance (ear clip + app). 20 minutes daily, morning after somatic release and Qi-Gong.

Real-time cardiac coherence training. High coherence reflects robust vagal tone and lower baseline sympathetic activation. Protocol: Breathe at 6 breaths/min (5-sec inhale, 5-sec exhale). Simultaneously recall a genuinely felt positive emotion — physically felt, not conceptually invoked. The breathing alone is insufficient. The emotion is what produces coherence.

⚑ App note — important: Always use Free Session (Quick Coherence) mode. The guided audio programs contain narrator speech that is a direct misophonia trigger — never use them. Always run through headphones, never phone speaker. The biofeedback loop is fully functional in Free Session mode; nothing is lost. Alternative: the Elite HRV app is compatible with the Inner Balance sensor and offers fully customizable audio.

Phase 1 · 4G

Safe and Sound Protocol (SSP)

Begins Month 2, Weeks 3–4. Platform: Unyte-iLs (licensed provider required). Total: 5 hours over 1–2 weeks.

Retrains the auditory safety detection circuits at hardware level — before the threat cascade fires. The stapedius muscle in chronic hypervigilance is undertrained. SSP exercises it and its vagal circuits. SSP does not do what CC does — they are complementary.

  • Lie down or sit comfortably. Headphones — not speakers. No multitasking.
  • On SSP days: no standalone CC sessions. tDCS continues normally.
  • Kava before an SSP session is acceptable if anticipatory anxiety is present.
  • After course: 2–3 days before pushing CC hierarchy.

A mandatory SSP refresher runs in Phase 4 from the SGB-reset baseline, where it lands at far greater depth. The primary course primes the system; the refresher rewrites it.

Section 5 — Community-Life Containment
Section 5 · 5A

Meals

The nervous system does not distinguish between a CC session and daily life. Every communal meal is a data point — either reinforcing the old circuit or failing to add to it.

Pre-entry: Green/Yellow/Red check. On Red days, solo meal is not avoidance — it is appropriate protection.

Seating: Edge or corner, back to wall, clear exit access.

Timing: Slightly early or late to avoid seating scramble.

Sound support: Bone conduction pink noise as default. Earplugs as backup. Both simultaneously is acceptable.

Duration: No obligation to stay. A short, complete meal with a clean exit is a regulated experience. Forcing a prolonged stay when escalating is reinforcement, not practice.

Phase 2 · 6A

Counterconditioning

CC is not exposure therapy. Exposure relies on habituation. The misophonic circuit fires faster than top-down regulation can reach it — passive exposure can deepen it. CC actively pairs the trigger with a genuinely felt positive emotional state. Two non-negotiable conditions: (1) Positive state must be physically felt, not conceptually invoked. (2) Entry condition met: SUDs 0–2, body tension below 4/10.

Technique 1 — Neural Repatterning (primary technique)

Technique 1

Neural Repatterning

Entry: SUDs 0–2 via 3–5 min resonance breathing (6 breaths/min: 5-sec inhale, 5-sec exhale)

Step 1 — Priming (2–3 min): Positive playlist. Allow emotion to genuinely build in the body. Do not proceed until physically present.

Step 2 — Dual exposure (2–4 min): Positive music in dominant ear. Trigger sound at low volume in non-dominant ear. ~70% attention to positive, ~30% to trigger. Redirect gently when pulled.

Step 3 — Recovery (1–2 min): Trigger off. Positive music only.

Step 4 — Rest (2 min): Silence or ambient sound. Repeat 4–6 times.

Advance criterion: 4 consecutive reps at SUDs ≤2 consistently across multiple sessions → move one level up hierarchy.

Paired stimuli library, scheduling, and when CC isn't working

Paired stimuli library (build before first session):

  • Music: 3–5 pieces producing genuine positive emotion (chills, warmth, uplift). 30–40 min playlist. Test weekly. Replace anything that stops working.
  • Memory anchor: One vivid specific positive memory accessible within 30 seconds. Specific sensory detail. Rehearse until automatic.
  • Trigger library: Recordings by hierarchy level. Sources: Freesound.org, FOAMS database (Zenodo), yt-dlp. 3–5 distinct recordings per trigger type per band.

Scheduling: 3–4 sessions/week, Green days only. Not on Yellow days. Day 1 after macrodose is rest; CC resumes Day 2. Best timing: early afternoon. On waterfall days: ~2 hours after returning (BDNF spike).

When not going well: SUDs consistently ending higher → drop one level, do not push. Positive stimulus stops working mid-session → stop immediately; continuing is exposure without CC. Three+ consecutive sessions ending higher → Tactical Brake indicator.

Phase 2 · 6D

ACT — Acceptance and Commitment Therapy

Begins Phase 2. Self-directed daily. Resource: The Happiness Trap (Russ Harris).

ACT addresses the secondary suffering layer: distress about having the distress. It does not reduce the trigger response — CC does that. It makes the period before CC completes its work livable.

Defusion practice (daily, 2–3 min): When any trigger-related thought arises, prefix it silently: "I notice I am having the thought that..." Creates distance without suppression. The more automatic it becomes, the more useful it is.

Values-based action (weekly, one instance): One action consistent with who you want to be that requires tolerating some discomfort. The core ACT move: action in accordance with values despite discomfort, not elimination of it.

Phase 2 · 6E

Anti-Rumination Protocol

The anticipatory and post-event loops can consume more nervous system bandwidth than the actual triggers. Left unaddressed, rumination keeps the threat circuit primed continuously.

1. Pattern Interrupt — immediate: Stand up, change room, cold water, 10-sec shaking, or physiological sigh. Break the loop first. Content doesn't need addressing in the moment.

2. ACT Defusion — within 60 sec: "I notice I am rehearsing the scenario where..." Label it, let it be. Do not argue with, analyze, or try to resolve the content.

3. Time Container — for genuine processing: If the loop contains something real (not anxiety noise), give it 5–10 min: sitting, pen and paper, write it out. Close the notebook. Container is not permission to spiral.

Rumination increasing rather than decreasing is a Tactical Brake indicator.

Phase 3 · 7C

EMDR

2–3 sessions/week, post-CC, on CC days. Targets specific interpersonal trigger memories. Standard CC does not reach these directly. EMDR does.

Self-directed EMDR protocol

Use a bilateral audio app (alternating left-right tones at 1 Hz). Headphones required.

Step 1: Target one specific memory. Rate distress (SUDs 0–10).
Step 2: Activate memory vividly (sight, sound, physical sensation).
Step 3: Start bilateral audio. Hold memory. ~30-second sets. Notice what shifts. Do not direct.
Step 4: After each set, breath, note what came up, re-rate. If reducing → run another set.
Step 5: When distress reaches 0–2, install a positive cognition. Run 1–2 sets on it.
Grounding: 5 breaths, 5 things noticed, feet on floor. Never end with distress above 3/10.

Working with a trained EMDR therapist is preferable for high-charge memories. Self-directed appropriate for SUDs 4–7.

Avoid: Same day as macrodose or MDMA. During Tactical Brake. When baseline distress already elevated. Never start with highest-charge material.

Phase 3 · 7D

IFS — Internal Family Systems

1–2 self-directed sessions/week, post-trigger or post-EMDR.

The misophonic rage is not a malfunction. It is a protector part doing what it learned to do. Suppressing it hardens it. Meeting it with curiosity can dissolve the need for it from the inside.

Session (15–20 min): Quiet, eyes closed. Bring to mind recent trigger or felt sense of reactivity. Notice where it lives in the body. Ask: "What are you trying to protect me from?" Wait. Acknowledge. Ask: "What are you afraid would happen if you didn't react this way?" Write immediately after.

Section 10

Somatic Release Protocol

Phase 1 Version — Simplified (7–10 min, morning only)

Begins Week 3 of Phase 1.

Constructive rest (5 min): Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat, hands on lower belly. Lower back releases toward floor. Breathe into the hands. Jaw released, eyes soft.

Diaphragmatic reset (2–3 min): Inhale 4 counts into belly, pause 2, exhale 6 counts. Jaw and shoulders release on each exhale.

Phase 3 Onward — Full Sequence (20–25 min pre-CC; 10–15 min morning)

Constructive rest (5 min): On back, knees bent, feet flat, hands on belly. Lower back releases. Breathe in. Jaw released, eyes soft.

Psoas release (5 min): From constructive rest, allow one knee to slowly drop outward — do not force. Hold 2 min, return. Other side. The psoas connects to the diaphragm; its release directly reduces the brainstem's danger signal.

Hip circles (3 min): Standing, hands on hips. Slow circles 1 min each direction, then figure-eights.

Shoulder and neck release (3 min): Slow head semicircles forward only. Shoulder shrugs held 3 sec, released. Jaw drops, holds 5 sec, releases.

Diaphragmatic reset (3 min): Inhale 4 counts into belly, pause 2, exhale 6 counts with audible release. Jaw drops on each exhale.

Integration (2 min): Standing, notice body, shake out hands briefly, one slow breath.

Avoid: do not force sharp pain. Psoas release should feel like release, not stretch. If any position produces anxiety, remain in constructive rest.

Section 11

Yoga Nidra Protocol

What it is in this protocol: Thalamic filter training. In the theta-alpha boundary state (4–12 Hz, just above sleep onset), the thalamus remains active as a sensory filter while the cortex enters low arousal. Sounds register and pass without the cortical threat cascade being fully online. Distinct from HeartMath (vagal regulation from alert state) and CC (conscious association rewriting). Yoga Nidra trains what happens to sensory input in the passively filtered state — and does so through direct experience rather than external feedback, building the intuitive awareness the other practices support.

Protocol

Every evening, before sleep. Never skipped.

Phases 1–220-minute tracks
Phase 330–35-minute tracks
Phase 4 onward40–45-minute tracks

Track selection: Must include body scan, visualization, and open awareness of external sounds phases. Starting points: Jennifer Piercy "Yoga Nidra for Sleep," Huberman Lab NSDR, Ally Boothroyd. Identify 2–3 tracks that reliably produce the hypnagogic edge.

Open Awareness Phase

Do not redirect attention away from ambient sounds — including sounds from community spaces. Let them register. Label internally if helpful. Allow them to pass without bodily reaction. If a high-trigger sound intrudes and breaks the state: note it, return to breathing, re-enter the body scan. The recovery — the return to stillness after disruption — is itself the training. It is more valuable than an undisturbed session.

Night Waking

Do not check phone. Do not turn on light. Put on the 20-minute sleep-specific track. Maintains theta-alpha state even if sleep does not return, preventing the cortisol-driven frustration loop that raises the next day's reactive baseline.

Section 12

Counterconditioning Techniques

Techniques 1 and 3 are formal session techniques requiring entry conditions: SUDs 0–2, body tension below 4/10, resonance breathing to baseline, somatic release completed. Techniques 2, 4, 5, and 6 are ambient tools used in daily life — no formal entry requirements.

Technique 1 · Phase 2 onward · Formal session

Neural Repatterning — Primary Technique

Full protocol in Section 6A. Used in all phases throughout.

Technique 2 · Phase 2 onward · Ambient

Attention Training

When a trigger occurs in daily life: label it neutrally — "chewing," "breathing," "tapping." Just the label, without evaluation or narrative. Trains attentional system to register sound as information. Especially useful during Yoga Nidra's open awareness phase.

Technique 3 · Phase 3 onward · Formal session

Imaginal CC

The trigger sound is imagined rather than played — useful for highly interpersonal triggers where a recording cannot capture the full charge. Same structure as Technique 1 but with vividly imagined trigger. Requires more internal regulation capacity — appropriate from Phase 3.

Technique 4 · Phase 2 onward · Ambient

Physiological Sigh Interrupt

When a trigger fires in daily life: double inhale through the nose (sniff, then top it off) followed by a long, complete exhale through the mouth. The fastest known manual reset of the autonomic state. Real-time interrupt only — not in formal sessions.

Technique 5 · Phase 3 onward · Experimental · Ambient

Deliberate Mimicry

Deliberately make the trigger sound yourself. Motor-level reframe: from "something being done to me" to "something I do." Trial and assess — some find this immediately useful; others find it counterproductive.

Technique 6 · Phase 3 onward · Ambient

Cognitive Reappraisal

Generate a competing interpretation — genuinely possible, not forced positivity. "They are eating because they are hungry." Must be believable to the nervous system. Pair with Technique 4 for compound effect. Addresses the interpersonal-violation layer specifically.

Tracking & Testing

Tracking serves the protocol. The protocol is calibrated primarily by felt sense. Data is a secondary confirmation tool. Any tracking that feels like a burden: simplify or drop.

Weekly (Sunday, 10 min)

  • Trigger hierarchy re-rating: Re-rate the full hierarchy. Shift from baseline to current is the most direct measure of progress.
  • Felt-sense note: One sentence on how the week felt. Not analysis — just record.

Monthly

Avoidance inventory: List every situation still being avoided or heavily managed. The shrinking of this list over quarters is the most meaningful functional outcome measure.

Legal & Safety

Legal and Safety Note

Psilocybin and MDMA are Schedule I substances in the United States. Hawaii has no state-level decriminalization. Know your legal context before proceeding.

Hard contraindications for psilocybin and MDMA: Personal or family history of psychosis, schizophrenia, or bipolar I; current SSRIs, SNRIs, pharmaceutical lithium, or MAOIs; heart conditions; pregnancy.

Stellate Ganglion Block requires an experienced physician and full medical history review. Insist on ultrasound guidance.
