Glossary

Every term, supplement, food, device, technique, and concept in the protocol — what it is and why it's here. Click any underlined term to jump to its entry.

#
5-HT2A ReceptorConcept
A serotonin receptor subtype that is the primary molecular target of classical psychedelics including psilocybin (via its active metabolite psilocin). Activation of 5-HT2A receptors in the prefrontal cortex suppresses the default mode network, promotes dendritic spine growth, and increases neuroplasticity.
Protocol role: The core pharmacological mechanism through which psilocybin produces its neuroplasticity and therapeutic effects. 5-HT2A agonism is distinct from serotonin reuptake inhibition (SSRIs) — which is why psilocybin produces acute neuroplasticity that SSRIs do not.
5-HTP (Post-MDMA Protocol)Supplement
5-Hydroxytryptophan — the direct precursor to serotonin. Taken for 3 days after MDMA sessions to replenish serotonin stores.
Protocol role: 100mg daily for 3 days starting 24h after MDMA session only. Do not take in 24h before any psilocybin or MDMA session.
A
A-MISO-SMeasurement
Amsterdam Misophonia Scale — Severity. A 6-item self-report questionnaire measuring misophonia severity 0–24. Scores: 0–4 subclinical, 5–9 mild, 10–14 moderate, 15–19 severe, 20–24 extreme.
Protocol role: Primary outcome measure. Baseline at Phase 0 = 18 (severe). A 25% reduction (~13–14) is the Phase 2 benchmark. Measured monthly.
ACT DefusionTechnique
An ACT technique. When a trigger thought or ruminative loop begins, prefix it: "I notice I'm having the thought that…" Labels the thought as mental content rather than reality, creating distance without suppression.
Protocol role: Phase 2 onward. Ambient tool used in daily life, not formal sessions. By Phase 3 the prefix arrives before the spiral gains momentum.
ACT — Acceptance and Commitment TherapyConcept
A cognitive-behavioural approach that targets psychological flexibility. Core principle: suffering arises from fusion with thoughts and experiential avoidance. The goal is not to eliminate difficult thoughts but to change your relationship with them.
Protocol role: Used in this protocol for defusion and the anti-rumination protocol. See also: Defusion.
ActivaDose tDCSDevice
A transcranial direct current stimulation device delivering precise low-level direct current (1.5–2.0 mA) to scalp electrodes. Research-grade mA accuracy — consumer devices vary widely in actual current delivery.
Protocol role: Phase 3 onward. Anodal electrode at F3 (left dlPFC), reference above right eyebrow. 20 minutes immediately before each CC session. Upregulates prefrontal cortical excitability, accelerating fear extinction.
AdaptogenConcept
A class of herbal substances that help the body adapt to stress by modulating the HPA axis and sympathetic nervous system response. True adaptogens are bidirectional — they blunt overactivation without suppressing necessary stress responses.
Protocol role: KSM-66 ashwagandha and Bacopa are the primary adaptogens in this protocol. Rhodiola was considered and excluded as redundant with ashwagandha.
ALA — Alpha-Linolenic AcidConcept
A plant-based omega-3 fatty acid found in walnuts, flaxseed, and chia. A precursor to EPA and DHA, but conversion efficiency in the body is low (typically under 10%). ALA is a valuable dietary omega-3 but cannot replace EPA/DHA from krill oil and fish oil at the doses this protocol requires.
Protocol role: Provided by ground flaxseed and walnuts daily. The conversion limitation is why supplement sources (krill oil + omega-3 top-up) are necessary alongside these foods. ALA still contributes anti-inflammatory signalling independent of its EPA/DHA conversion.
Alpha WavesConcept
Brainwaves at 8–12 Hz — the relaxed-alert state. Present during calm, eyes-closed wakefulness. The upper boundary of the theta-alpha state targeted by Yoga Nidra. Associated with reduced anxiety and enhanced creative cognition.
Protocol role: Yoga Nidra produces sustained alpha-theta boundary states. Alpha production is also enhanced by HeartMath cardiac coherence training.
AMPK — AMP-Activated Protein KinaseConcept
A cellular energy sensor activated when energy is low (fasting, exercise, caloric restriction). Triggers cellular autophagy, mitochondrial biogenesis, and metabolic efficiency improvements. Sometimes called the "master metabolic switch."
Protocol role: Activated by intermittent fasting (aerobic day eating window), Zone 2 exercise, apple cider vinegar, and berberine (Phase 5). AMPK activation is why fasting before aerobic exercise amplifies BDNF and neuroplasticity benefits.
AmygdalaConcept
An almond-shaped region deep in the temporal lobe that functions as the brain's threat detector. It receives sensory input and rapidly triggers the fight-or-flight response before the cortex can consciously evaluate the situation. In misophonia, the amygdala fires in response to trigger sounds faster than top-down regulation can intercept.
Protocol role: One of the three core neural substrates in misophonia. The right stellate ganglion has strong connections to the amygdala — which is why the right-side SGB injection specifically targets amygdala reactivity and HPA axis overdrive.
Anterior Cingulate Cortex (ACC)Concept
A brain region involved in error detection, emotional regulation, and amplifying signals between subcortical and cortical areas. In misophonia, the ACC amplifies the threat signal generated by the amygdala and insula, giving it its characteristic intensity.
Protocol role: The ACC is part of the three-substrate misophonic cascade: sensorimotor cortex (simulation) → insula/ACC (amplification) → autonomic nervous system (physiological response). CC sessions gradually reduce the gain the ACC applies to incoming trigger signals.
AnthocyaninConcept
A class of red-blue-purple pigment compounds found in berries, purple vegetables, and red cabbage. Anthocyanins cross the blood-brain barrier and upregulate BDNF expression, reduce neuroinflammation, and improve cerebrovascular blood flow.
Protocol role: Provided by daily blueberries and blackberries. The highest concentrations are in wild blueberries (available frozen from Costco). Anthocyanins are among the most evidence-supported dietary compounds for brain health.
Anticipatory DreadConcept
Pre-exposure anxiety that activates the threat cascade before any trigger sound occurs. The system pre-fires — keeping sympathetic activation chronically elevated regardless of whether a trigger is actually present.
Protocol role: Layer 3 of misophonic reactivity. Addressed by SGB (structural floor-lowering), HRV biofeedback, Yoga Nidra, tVNS, and kava. The most pervasive layer in daily community life at RB.
ApigeninSupplement
A plant flavonoid found in chamomile. Acts as a mild GABA-A receptor modulator, reducing nighttime cortisol and promoting sleep onset without suppressing REM or producing next-day sedation.
Protocol role: Sleep stack. 50mg at bedtime from Phase 1. Avoid on nights drinking alcohol (potentiates sedation).
Apple Cider Vinegar (ACV)Food
Raw, unfiltered ACV. AMPK activator and blood sugar regulator. Reduces post-meal glucose spikes, which otherwise blunt the BDNF response to exercise and meals.
Protocol role: Daily. 1 tablespoon before meals.
Ashwagandha (KSM-66)Supplement
A standardised root extract of Withania somnifera (8% withanolides). A true adaptogen that modulates the HPA axis — reducing cortisol output, improving stress resilience, and blunting chronic sympathetic over-activation. KSM-66 is the most clinically studied extract.
Protocol role: Phase 2 onward. 600mg morning with food. Continue on psilocybin macrodose days (no interaction). Skip on MDMA session days.
AstaxanthinConcept
A carotenoid antioxidant found in krill and certain algae, responsible for the pink-red colour of krill. A powerful antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent that crosses both the blood-brain barrier and the blood-retinal barrier. Present in krill oil (SUPERBABoost) alongside the phospholipid EPA/DHA.
Protocol role: An incidental benefit of krill oil use. Not a primary reason for krill selection (phospholipid delivery is), but the astaxanthin content adds meaningful antioxidant protection during intensive neuroplasticity work when oxidative load is elevated.
ATP — Adenosine TriphosphateConcept
The primary energy currency of every cell. Creatine (stored as phosphocreatine) is the brain's fastest-access energy buffer for rapid ATP resynthesis during demanding cognitive and sensory processing. Without adequate phosphocreatine stores, the brain's processing efficiency drops.
Protocol role: The reason creatine is the first supplement listed in the protocol and taken on waking. Plant-forward diets provide zero dietary creatine — supplementation is essential for maintaining brain creatine stores at the level required for intensive neuroplasticity work.
AutophagyConcept
A cellular self-cleaning process where cells break down and recycle damaged proteins and organelles. Essential for clearing the cellular debris that accumulates between intensive neuroplasticity events. Triggered by fasting, exercise, and spermidine.
Protocol role: Supported by the intermittent fasting window, Zone 2 exercise, and spermidine-rich foods (tempeh, wheat germ). Particularly relevant in Phase 4 where intensive neuroplasticity work generates significant cellular turnover.
Avoidance InventoryMeasurement
A quarterly self-assessment listing every situation currently being avoided or heavily managed due to misophonia. The shrinking of this list over quarters is the most meaningful functional outcome measure.
Protocol role: Quarterly. More meaningful than A-MISO-S as a real-world function measure.
B
B-Complex (Methylated)Supplement
A combination of all B vitamins in their active (methylated) forms — methylfolate rather than folic acid, methylcobalamin rather than cyanocobalamin. Supports methylation, neurotransmitter synthesis, and neurological function.
Protocol role: Phase 1 onward. 1 capsule morning. Morning only — B vitamins can increase alertness if taken late. Must show methylfolate and methylcobalamin on label.
B12 (Methylcobalamin Sublingual)Supplement
Vitamin B12 in its active form, dissolved under the tongue. Essential on plant-forward diets where dietary B12 is absent. Supports myelin integrity, nerve conduction, and red blood cell production.
Protocol role: Phase 1 onward. 1000mcg dissolved under tongue daily.
Bacopa (Synapsa)Supplement
A standardised extract of Bacopa monnieri. Specifically supports extinction learning — the type of associative learning CC sessions produce. Takes 4–6 weeks to reach full effect. Also improves working memory.
Protocol role: Phase 1 Week 3 onward. 320mg morning with food. The single supplement with the most directly relevant mechanistic evidence for CC-based rewriting.
BDNF — Brain-Derived Neurotrophic FactorConcept
A protein that supports neuronal growth, survival, and differentiation. Elevated BDNF increases synaptic plasticity — the ease with which new neural connections form and consolidate.
Protocol role: Raised by Zone 2 aerobic exercise, psilocybin, Lion's Mane, creatine, cold plunge, and intermittent fasting. The biological foundation that determines how effectively CC sessions write new associations. CC sessions on waterfall days are timed ~2 hours post-exercise to catch the BDNF spike.
BerberineSupplement
An alkaloid activating AMPK — the cellular energy sensor. Improves insulin sensitivity, regulates blood glucose, and supports metabolic health.
Protocol role: Phase 5 onward. 500mg midday 3x per week (not on resistance training days). Part of the cellular wellness layer.
Berries (Blueberries, Blackberries)Food
High-anthocyanin fruits. Anthocyanins upregulate BDNF expression. Wild blueberries have significantly higher polyphenol concentrations than cultivated varieties.
Protocol role: Daily. Costco frozen wild blueberries recommended.
Beta-BlockerConcept
A class of drugs that block adrenaline (epinephrine) and noradrenaline from binding to beta-adrenergic receptors. Reduces heart rate, blood pressure, and the peripheral symptoms of anxiety and stress arousal.
Protocol role: Propranolol is the beta-blocker used in this protocol — specifically for memory reconsolidation. Its mechanism here is not anxiolytic (reducing anxiety in the moment) but interference with the re-storage of fear memories during the reconsolidation window.
Beta-GlucanConcept
A polysaccharide found in the cell walls of medicinal mushrooms. The primary bioactive fraction responsible for Lion's Mane's NGF-stimulating effects. "Dual-extracted" mushroom products capture both water-soluble beta-glucans and fat-soluble active compounds (hericenones/erinacines). Products must state beta-glucan content to be considered valid.
Protocol role: The reason the protocol specifies dual-extracted Lion's Mane that declares beta-glucan content. Single-extraction or products without stated beta-glucan content are inadequate — the beta-glucan fraction is what distinguishes a therapeutic dose from inert mushroom powder.
Bilateral StimulationConcept
Alternating sensory stimulation (audio tones, eye movements, or tactile taps) switching between the left and right sides. In EMDR, bilateral stimulation applied during memory activation appears to engage the brain's innate memory processing mechanism.
Protocol role: Core mechanism of EMDR. In this protocol's self-directed EMDR, bilateral audio tones (headphones) are used. The alternating left-right activation may mimic the lateral eye movement processing that occurs during REM sleep.
Bone Conduction Headphones (Shokz OpenRun Pro 2)Device
Headphones transmitting audio via vibration through the cheekbones. Ear canals remain completely open — all ambient sound heard naturally. Cheekbone pathway sufficient for music and pink noise.
Protocol role: Phase 1 Day 1 onward. Used at all communal events (meetings, meals, car rides) for meeting support — low-volume pink noise occupies the thalamic scanning reflex without blocking situational awareness.
Brassicas (Broccoli, Cauliflower)Food
Cruciferous vegetables producing sulforaphane — which activates the Nrf2 pathway, a master regulator of antioxidant and anti-inflammatory gene expression.
Protocol role: Regularly. Chop and wait 40 minutes before cooking to allow enzymatic conversion. Add mustard powder when cooking to restore the deactivated enzyme.
BupivacaineSubstance
A long-acting local anaesthetic used in SGB procedures. Injected into the stellate ganglion region, it temporarily interrupts nerve conduction — specifically the overactive NGF signalling driving chronic sympathetic hyperactivation.
Protocol role: The pharmacological agent in SGB. Its effects at the stellate ganglion are typically described as lasting hours, but the sympathetic reset it triggers can persist months to years — far beyond the drug's pharmacological half-life.
C
CapersFood
Highest quercetin concentration of any food (~20mg per tablespoon). Quercetin is a senolytic that selectively clears senescent cells driving chronic inflammation.
Protocol role: Daily. Add to salads, dressings, pasta — effortless at this level.
Cardiac CoherenceConcept
A state in which heart rate oscillations become highly ordered and rhythmically regular — producing a smooth, sine-wave-like pattern in the HRV signal. Achieved through resonance frequency breathing (6 bpm) combined with genuine positive emotional recall. High coherence reflects maximal vagal tone engagement.
Protocol role: The target state of HeartMath sessions. The coherence score is displayed in real time by the Inner Balance app. The breathing rhythm alone produces some coherence — the genuine emotion is what produces high coherence. HeartMath's core insight is that the heart's electrical field communicates bidirectionally with the brain.
CatechinConcept
A family of plant-derived antioxidant compounds found in green tea. EGCG (epigallocatechin gallate) is the most potent catechin in green tea. Catechins have anti-inflammatory, neuroprotective, and antioxidant effects. The primary catechin delivery vehicle in this protocol is matcha (daily) and green tea (afternoon).
Protocol role: Present in both matcha and green tea. Matcha has significantly higher catechin content than steeped green tea because the whole leaf is consumed as powder. The catechin EGCG is also used as a standalone supplement post-MDMA for serotonin protection.
CC — CounterconditioningConcept
The primary behavioural rewriting mechanism. A trigger sound is presented at low volume while simultaneously inducing a genuinely felt positive emotional state. Repeated pairings build a competing neural association strong enough to inhibit the threat response.
Protocol role: The engine of the entire protocol. Everything else — supplements, SGB, psilocybin, devices — exists to open the window or support the quality of CC sessions. Six techniques used across phases.
Circadian RhythmConcept
The approximately 24-hour biological clock regulating sleep-wake cycles, hormone release, metabolism, and many other physiological processes. The circadian clock is primarily set by light exposure — morning bright light triggers the diurnal cortisol rise and anchors the sleep-wake cycle.
Protocol role: The sleep protocol is built around circadian hygiene: fixed wake time, bright light within 10 minutes of waking, final meal by 6pm, screens off by 9pm, sleep onset 9:30pm. Melatonin (Phase 1 only) is used as a temporary rhythm-setter. All of this determines whether the cortisol diurnal curve fires at 7am (correct) or 3am (problem).
Cognitive ReappraisalTechnique
Generating a competing, genuinely believable interpretation of a trigger situation: "They are eating because they are hungry" rather than "They are doing this to me." Must be plausible to the nervous system — forced positivity does not work.
Protocol role: Technique 6. Addresses the social threat coding layer (Layer 2). Pairs with Technique 4 (physiological sigh interrupt) for compound effect in daily life.
Cold PlungeConcept
Immersion in cold water (waterfall or cold shower) for 3–5 minutes. Activates norepinephrine release, which enhances focus and mood for several hours. Also triggers a sympathetic-then-parasympathetic rebound that lowers resting arousal baseline.
Protocol role: Integrated into waterfall days (Zone 2 aerobic + cold plunge sequence). CC sessions scheduled approximately 2 hours post-plunge to align with the BDNF and norepinephrine spike.
Convergence WindowConcept
A period when multiple plasticity-enhancing mechanisms are active simultaneously — SGB floor-lowering, post-psilocybin neuroplasticity window, post-exercise BDNF elevation, and SSP auditory recalibration. The overlap creates optimal conditions for deep associative rewriting.
Protocol role: Phase 4 is engineered around the Convergence Window. This is why Phase 4 requires dedicated reduced-exposure time away from the daily trigger environment.
CortisolConcept
The primary stress hormone, produced by the adrenal glands under HPA axis stimulation. Chronically elevated cortisol impairs hippocampal neurogenesis, disrupts sleep architecture, reduces HRV, and maintains a high sympathetic floor.
Protocol role: Tracked via salivary cortisol panel (4-point diurnal test) at Phase 0 baseline and Phase 4 repeat. Reduced by ashwagandha, phosphatidylserine, adequate sleep, somatic release, and HRV biofeedback training.
Creatine Monohydrate (Creapure)Supplement
A compound stored in muscle and brain tissue as phosphocreatine — a rapid-resynthesis energy buffer for ATP. In the brain, creatine supports energy metabolism during demanding cognitive work. Plant-forward diets have zero dietary creatine.
Protocol role: Phase 1 onward. 7.5g on waking in water. Critical given plant-forward diet. Creapure® is the German-manufactured purity standard.
D
D3 / K2Supplement
Vitamin D3 (a neuroactive hormone) paired with Vitamin K2 (MK-7 form, which directs calcium to bones rather than arteries). D3 supports neuroplasticity, immune regulation, and mood.
Protocol role: Phase 1 onward. 2000–5000 IU D3 + 100mcg K2 MK-7, morning with fat-containing food.
Dark Chocolate (85%+)Food
High-cocoa chocolate containing theobromine (mild vasodilator) and flavanols (anti-inflammatory, cerebrovascular health).
Protocol role: Daily. 85%+ only — lower cocoa percentage means substantially less flavanol and more sugar.
Default Mode Network (DMN)Concept
A large-scale brain network most active during self-referential thought, mind-wandering, and anticipation. In chronic misophonia, the DMN maintains ongoing anticipatory activation — the resting threat state between actual triggers.
Protocol role: Psilocybin temporarily suppresses DMN activity, enabling the threat circuit to be approached without the usual top-down narrative reinforcement. Yoga Nidra also reduces DMN activity.
DefusionConcept
An ACT technique for separating the observing self from automatic thought content. Rather than suppressing or arguing with a looping thought, you label it: "I notice I'm having the thought that…" The thought is acknowledged without being acted upon.
Protocol role: Core anti-rumination tool (Section 6D/6E). Becomes increasingly automatic across phases — by Phase 3 the prefix arrives before the spiral gains momentum.
dlPFC — Dorsolateral Prefrontal CortexConcept
A region of the frontal lobe involved in top-down emotional regulation, working memory, and executive control. Stronger left dlPFC activation correlates with faster fear extinction and better override of automatic threat responses.
Protocol role: The target of tDCS anodal stimulation (electrode at F3 position). Upregulating dlPFC excitability before CC sessions increases the prefrontal "brake" on the amygdala threat cascade.
DopamineConcept
A neurotransmitter involved in motivation, reward, movement, and learning. In the context of neuroplasticity, dopamine signals "this is worth remembering" — it strengthens the encoding of experiences that occurred alongside it. MDMA causes large dopamine release alongside serotonin and norepinephrine.
Protocol role: Relevant to CC sessions: the genuine positive emotional state required for counterconditioning involves dopaminergic reward circuits. Psilocybin and MDMA both interact with dopamine pathways. The protocol's physical practices (exercise, cold plunge) also elevate dopamine.
DSR — Dual Sympathetic ResetConcept
The bilateral Stellate Ganglion Block protocol. Right stellate injected first (suppresses amygdala reactivity and HPA overdrive), left side 24–72 hours later (restores systemic autonomic balance). Together they produce a bilateral reset of the sympathetic baseline.
Protocol role: The Phase 0 structural intervention. Also used at Phase 4 opening. Provider must be experienced with ultrasound-guided SGB.
E
EGCG (Post-MDMA)Supplement
Epigallocatechin gallate — the primary catechin in green tea. As a standalone supplement, used post-MDMA for 3 days as part of serotonin protection protocol. Daily source is matcha and green tea.
Protocol role: 400mg for 3 days beginning 24h after MDMA sessions. Also present in matcha (daily) and green tea (afternoon) throughout the protocol.
EGCG — Epigallocatechin GallateConcept
The most abundant and biologically active catechin in green tea. Anti-inflammatory (inhibits COX-2 and NF-κB pathways), neuroprotective, antioxidant, and mildly thermogenic. Present in high concentrations in matcha and moderate concentrations in steeped green tea. Used as a standalone supplement post-MDMA for serotonin protection.
Protocol role: Daily source: matcha (morning) and green tea (afternoon). The post-MDMA protocol uses 400mg supplemental EGCG for 3 days to support serotonin protection alongside 5-HTP. Space both drinks 1 hour from iron-rich meals — EGCG inhibits non-heme iron absorption.
EMDR — Eye Movement Desensitization and ReprocessingTechnique
A therapy that pairs activation of a distressing memory with bilateral stimulation (alternating audio tones or eye movements). This appears to activate the brain's innate memory processing mechanism — the same one operating during REM sleep — allowing the memory to reprocess with reduced emotional charge.
Protocol role: Phase 3 onward. Targets the interpersonal-violation layer — specific incidents with specific people. Self-directed using bilateral audio tones. 2–3 sessions per week, post-CC on CC days.
EPA / DHAConcept
Eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA) and docosahexaenoic acid (DHA) — the two long-chain omega-3 fatty acids with direct neurological importance. DHA is the dominant fatty acid in brain grey matter and myelin. EPA has strong anti-inflammatory properties. Both are required for neuronal membrane integrity and the intensive synaptic remodelling this protocol produces.
Protocol role: The combined EPA+DHA daily target in this protocol is 2–3g from krill oil + triglyceride top-up. Krill delivers them in phospholipid form (superior neural membrane incorporation); the top-up contributes dose adequacy. Both can be skipped on oily fish days.
ErgothioneineConcept
A naturally occurring amino acid found almost exclusively in fungi. Consistently associated with reduced all-cause mortality and reduced disease risk in large-scale blood analyses. Uniquely accumulated in certain human tissues (liver, kidneys, eyes, brain) via a specific transporter. Heat-stable — cooking does not degrade it.
Protocol role: Provided by daily mushroom consumption. The specific transporter that accumulates ergothioneine in human tissue suggests it may serve a cellular protective role the body actively preserves. Oyster, porcini, and shiitake mushrooms have the highest concentrations.
ErinacinesConcept
Fat-soluble active compounds found in Lion's Mane mushroom (Hericium erinaceus). Erinacines stimulate NGF (Nerve Growth Factor) synthesis inside the central nervous system — they cross the blood-brain barrier and directly promote neuronal growth and maintenance.
Protocol role: The reason Lion's Mane must be dual-extracted (requiring both water and alcohol/ethanol extraction). Water extraction yields beta-glucans; ethanol/fat extraction yields erinacines. Single-extraction products miss one of the two primary active fractions.
Extinction LearningConcept
The neural process by which a previously conditioned fear or threat response diminishes through repeated non-reinforced exposure. Distinct from counterconditioning — extinction suppresses the original response; CC builds a competing one.
Protocol role: Bacopa specifically supports extinction learning. Propranolol-assisted reconsolidation targets the memory trace directly rather than suppressing it.

Felt-Sense GateConcept
A phase transition criterion based on embodied, subjective readiness rather than calendar duration. A phase gate is passed when the current phase's tools feel genuinely familiar and no longer effortful as a whole.
Protocol role: Every phase transition uses felt-sense gates. The principle: a phase not fully absorbed cannot support what the next phase asks of it.
Fermented Foods (Kimchi, Sauerkraut, Miso)Food
Live-culture fermented foods supporting microbiome diversity, which is strongly associated with mental health and vagal tone via the gut-brain axis.
Protocol role: Regularly. Diversity of strains matters more than quantity of any single strain.
FisetinConcept
A plant polyphenol flavonoid found in highest concentrations in strawberries. A potent senolytic — it selectively induces apoptosis (programmed cell death) in senescent cells while leaving healthy cells intact.
Protocol role: Provided by weekly strawberry consumption. One of the most potent senolytics identified in common foods. The protocol emphasises strawberries in Phase 5 as part of the cellular wellness layer.
FlavanolsConcept
A subclass of flavonoids found in cocoa, tea, and apples. Flavanols support cerebrovascular health by promoting nitric oxide production in blood vessel walls, improving cerebral blood flow, and reducing oxidative stress. The higher the cocoa content in dark chocolate, the higher the flavanol density.
Protocol role: Provided by daily dark chocolate (85%+) and green tea. The minimum 85% cocoa threshold in the protocol is specifically to ensure meaningful flavanol content — milk chocolate and lower-cocoa varieties have substantially less.
FOAMS DatabaseMeasurement
Free Online Acoustic/Misophonia Stimuli — a validated database of trigger sounds on Zenodo (doi: 10.5281/zenodo.4813431). Standardised audio files of common misophonic triggers at calibrated volumes.
Protocol role: Used for CC session audio library construction.
G
GABA — Gamma-Aminobutyric AcidConcept
The primary inhibitory neurotransmitter in the central nervous system. GABA reduces neuronal excitability, producing calming effects. GABA-A receptors are the most common type and are the target of benzodiazepines, alcohol, and several natural compounds including apigenin and kavalactones.
Protocol role: The GABAergic system is a key target in the anxiety/anticipatory layer of misophonia. Apigenin (sleep stack) modulates GABA-A mildly. Kava's kavalactones have complex GABAergic activity. Understanding GABA explains why these compounds reduce anticipatory activation without cognitive impairment.
GABA-A ReceptorConcept
A type of inhibitory receptor in the nervous system activated by the neurotransmitter GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid). GABA-A activation produces calming, anxiolytic, and sedative effects. Many sleep aids and anti-anxiety medications work via this receptor.
Protocol role: Apigenin is a mild GABA-A receptor modulator used in the sleep stack. Kava's kavalactones also have GABA-A activity alongside their kavalactone-specific effects. Understanding this receptor explains why these compounds promote calm without sedating the way pharmaceuticals do.
GlutamateConcept
The primary excitatory neurotransmitter in the central nervous system. Glutamate dysregulation drives compulsive and ruminative thought patterns — the same neural signature underlying OCD, addiction, and the anticipatory-dread aspect of misophonia. NMDA receptors are a key glutamate subtype.
Protocol role: NAC (N-Acetyl Cysteine) in the supplement stack is included specifically for its glutamate-modulating effects — it restores cystine-glutamate exchange at synapses, reducing the compulsive and ruminative quality of the anticipatory layer.
GlycineSupplement
An amino acid that lowers core body temperature at sleep onset and improves sleep quality. Taken as a powder in water.
Protocol role: Sleep stack. 3g at bedtime from Phase 1. Works via a distinct mechanism from magnesium and apigenin — the combination is genuinely additive.
Green Tea / KukichaFood
Lower-caffeine afternoon tea. Provides EGCG and trace L-theanine. Space 1 hour from iron-rich meals (EGCG inhibits non-heme iron absorption).
Protocol role: Afternoon daily. Kukicha is particularly low in caffeine — well-suited to late-afternoon use given the early sleep target.
Ground FlaxseedFood
Provides ALA (plant omega-3 precursor), lignans, and soluble fibre. ALA conversion efficiency to EPA/DHA is low — supplement sources (krill + omega-3 top-up) are required for protocol doses.
Protocol role: Daily. 1–2 tablespoons ground (not whole). Add to smoothies, oats, or salads.
Gut-Brain AxisConcept
The bidirectional communication network between the gut microbiome and the brain, mediated primarily through the vagus nerve, immune signalling, and neurotransmitter precursor production. The gut produces approximately 95% of the body's serotonin. Microbiome diversity is strongly associated with mental health, stress resilience, and vagal tone.
Protocol role: Supported by L. reuteri, fermented foods (kimchi, sauerkraut, miso), and fibre-rich plant foods. L. reuteri specifically stimulates oxytocin via vagal signalling — a direct gut-to-brain neurological effect, not just microbiome composition.
H
HeartMath Inner BalanceDevice
An ear-clip photoplethysmography sensor connected to the HeartMath app. Measures beat-to-beat heart rate variation in real time and displays a cardiac coherence score. High coherence reflects coordinated heart-brain communication via the vagus nerve.
Protocol role: Phase 1 Month 2 onward. Daily 20-minute sessions. Always use Free Session (Quick Coherence) mode — the guided audio programs contain narrator speech that is a direct misophonia trigger. Always use headphones, never phone speaker.
HericenonesConcept
Water-soluble active compounds found in the fruiting body of Lion's Mane mushroom. Hericenones stimulate NGF (Nerve Growth Factor) synthesis in peripheral nervous tissue. They complement erinacines (which act centrally) by working at the peripheral level.
Protocol role: The water-soluble fraction captured by the water extraction in dual-extracted Lion's Mane. Together with erinacines (fat-soluble fraction), they provide both central and peripheral NGF stimulation.
Hippocampal NeurogenesisConcept
The production of new neurons in the hippocampus — one of the few brain regions where adult neurogenesis occurs. New hippocampal neurons are essential for encoding new extinction memories and for contextual learning (learning that a previously threatening stimulus is safe in a new context).
Protocol role: Supported by lithium orotate, BDNF-raising interventions (exercise, psilocybin), adequate sleep, and intermittent fasting. Chronically elevated cortisol suppresses hippocampal neurogenesis — one reason the HPA axis work in this protocol is foundational.
HippocampusConcept
A brain structure critical for forming new memories, spatial navigation, and contextual fear learning. The hippocampus is where extinction memories are stored — it tells the amygdala "this stimulus is safe now, in this context."
Protocol role: Low cortisol and adequate sleep are essential for hippocampal neurogenesis. Lithium orotate, BDNF-raising interventions, and psilocybin all support hippocampal function. Extinction learning (the goal of CC) depends on healthy hippocampal encoding.
Horner's SyndromeConcept
A temporary cluster of symptoms on one side of the face: slight eyelid drooping (ptosis), pupil constriction (miosis), and mild flushing. A normal and expected post-SGB finding confirming the block reached the stellate ganglion.
Protocol role: Resolves within hours. Its presence is reassuring — it means the anaesthetic reached the target. If Horner's syndrome does not appear after an SGB injection, the block may have been suboptimal — worth noting for the provider.
HPA Axis — Hypothalamic-Pituitary-AdrenalConcept
The hormonal stress-response system. The hypothalamus signals the pituitary, which signals the adrenal glands to release cortisol. Chronic activation keeps baseline arousal elevated and impairs fear extinction.
Protocol role: Ashwagandha (KSM-66) is the primary HPA axis modulator in the protocol. SGB indirectly reduces HPA overdrive by interrupting stellate ganglion NGF signalling. Salivary cortisol panel tests the diurnal HPA rhythm.
HRV TrendMeasurement
The weekly pattern of heart rate variability measurements. An upward trend over 4+ consecutive weeks confirms vagal tone is building.
Protocol role: Weekly monitoring throughout all phases via HeartMath coherence scores and the longitudinal HRV tracking method selected in Section 4E.
HRV — Heart Rate VariabilityConcept
The variation in time between consecutive heartbeats. High HRV reflects robust vagal tone and parasympathetic dominance. Low HRV indicates sympathetic dominance and reduced stress resilience.
Protocol role: Primary longitudinal physiological marker. Weekly trend confirms whether substrate work is building vagal tone. Tracked via HeartMath Inner Balance and a longitudinal tracking method (Section 4E).
Hypnagogic StateConcept
The transitional consciousness state at the boundary between wakefulness and sleep — characterised by theta-alpha brainwaves (4–12 Hz), vivid imagery, and deep physical relaxation while maintaining some awareness. The target state of Yoga Nidra.
Protocol role: Yoga Nidra is specifically designed to induce and sustain this state. The thalamus remains active as a sensory filter while cortical threat-processing is suppressed. Sounds experienced in the hypnagogic state — including trigger sounds — register and pass without the full cascade engaging.

IFS — Internal Family SystemsTechnique
A psychotherapy model treating the psyche as composed of distinct "parts" — each with its own perspective and protective function. In this protocol, IFS targets the protector part that generates the misophonic rage response — not to eliminate it but to understand what it is protecting.
Protocol role: Phase 3 onward. Complements EMDR (EMDR processes memories; IFS works the relational/protective structure underneath). Run after EMDR or as standalone on CC days.
Imaginal CC (Technique 3)Technique
A counterconditioning variant where the trigger sound is vividly imagined rather than played from a recording. Useful for highly interpersonal triggers where a recording cannot capture the full charge.
Protocol role: Phase 3 onward. Requires more internal regulation capacity than Technique 1. Most relevant for the most charged interpersonal triggers.
InsulaConcept
A region of the cerebral cortex involved in interoception, emotional awareness, and the subjective experience of bodily states. In misophonia, the anterior insula amplifies the sensorimotor threat signal into a full emotional response.
Protocol role: One of three neural substrates driving misophonic reactivity. CC and MDMA-assisted processing work partly by reducing insula reactivity. tDCS applied to left dlPFC modulates pathways that include insula activity.
Integration WeekConcept
The 7-day period following a psilocybin macrodose or MDMA session. Day 1: rest only — no CC, no demanding practices. Days 2–7: the Pulse — daily CC sessions capitalising on the peak neuroplasticity window. Sleep prioritised throughout. No alcohol, no disruptions where avoidable.
Protocol role: The most productive CC window of each cycle. The protocol schedules integration weeks to be as uninterrupted as possible. Phase 4's dedicated reduced-exposure setting is designed to make integration weeks maximally productive.
Intermittent FastingConcept
A time-restricted eating pattern where food intake is limited to a specific window. On aerobic days, first meal at 10am or later — maintaining a fasted state through the morning exercise. The fasting window activates AMPK, autophagy, and BDNF production.
Protocol role: Aerobic day eating window from Phase 1. Not applied on resistance training days (first meal immediately after training for protein synthesis). One of the highest-leverage metabolic interventions given it costs nothing and amplifies BDNF from exercise.
K
Kava (Noble)Substance
A traditional Pacific Islander anxiolytic made from Piper methysticum root. Active compounds (kavalactones) have GABAergic, dopaminergic, and ion-channel effects. Unlike alcohol, kava does not impair cognition, disrupt sleep, or produce next-day effects at traditional doses.
Protocol role: Phase 2 onward. 45–60 minutes before high-trigger contexts or challenging CC sessions. Maximum 3x per week. Noble kava root powder only — not instant, not extract, not tudei. Cool water preparation only (hot water degrades kavalactones). Avoid with alcohol. Role naturally diminishes as CC progresses and anticipatory layer reduces.
KavalactonesConcept
The primary bioactive compounds in kava root, responsible for its anxiolytic and muscle-relaxing effects. Kavalactones interact with multiple targets: GABA-A receptors (mild), dopamine pathways, ion channels, and the endocannabinoid system. They produce anxiolysis without sedation or cognitive impairment. Hot water degrades kavalactones — preparation with room-temperature water is essential.
Protocol role: The reason noble kava root powder is specified (high kavalactone content) and cool water is mandatory. Instant kava, kava extracts, and tudei kava all have inferior or altered kavalactone profiles.
Krill Oil (SUPERBABoost)Supplement
Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA and DHA) in phospholipid form — the delivery format that incorporates most efficiently into neuronal membranes. The only practical consumer source of phospholipid-form EPA/DHA.
Protocol role: Phase 1 onward. 2–3 softgels morning with food. Paired with omega-3 triglyceride top-up to reach the 2–3g combined EPA+DHA daily target. Pause 1 week before SGB. Skip on oily fish days.
L
L-TheanineConcept
An amino acid found in green tea, particularly matcha, in concentrations of 300–400mg per cup. Promotes alpha-wave activity and calm alertness by partially blocking excitatory glutamate receptors and increasing GABA and dopamine. When paired with caffeine, it smooths the stimulant effect into focused calm without the cortisol spike.
Protocol role: Present in daily matcha (morning) and green tea (afternoon). This is why L-theanine was removed as a standalone supplement — daily matcha already provides a higher baseline dose than supplement capsules. The compound earns its place as a food-based delivery.
L. reuteri (BioGaia Gastrus)Supplement
Lactobacillus reuteri — a probiotic strain that stimulates oxytocin production via vagal nerve signalling. Also supports gut microbiome diversity and gut-brain axis communication.
Protocol role: Phase 1 onward. 1–2 tablets chewed thoroughly on waking. Chew — do not swallow whole. Keep refrigerated.

Locus CoeruleusConcept
A nucleus in the brainstem that is the brain's primary norepinephrine-producing region. Activated by tVNS via the auricular branch of the vagus nerve. Norepinephrine release from the locus coeruleus enhances the durability of newly formed memories.
Protocol role: The mechanistic pathway through which tVNS enhances CC session efficacy: vagal stimulation → locus coeruleus activation → norepinephrine release → enhanced plasticity and memory consolidation during the simultaneous CC pairing.

MDMASubstance
3,4-methylenedioxymethamphetamine. A psychoactive that produces simultaneous serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine release alongside oxytocin. In therapeutic settings, MDMA reduces activity in the fear centre (amygdala) while preserving conscious access to difficult memories — enabling processing of the interpersonal-violation layer that is inaccessible in ordinary states.
Protocol role: Session 1 in Phase 3; Session 2 in Phase 4. Targets Layer 2 (social threat coding) specifically. Schedule I in the US — know your legal context. Post-session: 5-HTP 100mg + EGCG 400mg for 3 days beginning 24h after, to replenish serotonin.
Melatonin (Low Dose)Supplement
A hormone that signals darkness and regulates circadian rhythm onset. At 0.5mg, as effective as higher doses for sleep onset with fewer side effects.
Protocol role: Phase 1 only, first 4–6 weeks. Temporary rhythm-setter only. Taper and discontinue — the sleep protocol is the long-term mechanism.
Memory ReconsolidationConcept
Every time a memory is retrieved, it temporarily becomes malleable — it must be "reconsolidated" to persist. This window can be exploited: if a destabilising agent (propranolol, MDMA) is introduced during retrieval, the memory reconsolidates with reduced emotional charge.
Protocol role: The mechanism behind propranolol-assisted reconsolidation (Section 7E). The trigger memory is deliberately activated, then propranolol prevents the threat response from being stamped back in at full intensity.
MethylationConcept
A biochemical process involving the transfer of a methyl group (CH₃) to DNA, proteins, or other molecules. In the context of B vitamins: methylation supports neurotransmitter synthesis (serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine), DNA repair, and detoxification. Impaired methylation (due to MTHFR gene variants, folate deficiency, or B12 deficiency) reduces all of these.
Protocol role: The reason the B-complex supplement specifically requires methylated forms — methylfolate (not folic acid) and methylcobalamin (not cyanocobalamin). These are the active forms that enter methylation pathways directly, bypassing conversion steps that may be impaired.
Mirror Neuron MechanismConcept
The process by which the brain involuntarily simulates the motor action producing a sound — the sensorimotor cortex activates as if the listener's body were performing the action. In misophonia, this simulation cascades into a threat response.
Protocol role: Identified in Kumar et al. (2021) as the motor basis of misophonia. Explains why eating sounds feel like a violation — the brain is simulating the eating action.
Mitochondrial BiogenesisConcept
The process by which cells generate new mitochondria — the cellular organelles responsible for ATP production. Zone 2 aerobic exercise is the most potent stimulator of mitochondrial biogenesis. More and better mitochondria mean more efficient energy production for neuronal activity and neuroplasticity.
Protocol role: The deep reason Zone 2 exercise is structural in this protocol — not just for BDNF, but for the long-term improvement of the brain's energy production infrastructure. AMPK activation (via exercise, fasting, berberine) triggers PGC-1α, the master regulator of mitochondrial biogenesis.
Mushrooms (Daily)Food
Any culinary mushrooms provide ergothioneine — consistently associated with reduced all-cause mortality. Oyster, porcini, and shiitake are highest; any variety contributes. Heat-stable.
Protocol role: Daily. Growing your own (NorthSpore kits) is ideal. Powder form acceptable.
MyelinConcept
The fatty insulating sheath surrounding nerve axons, produced by oligodendrocytes in the central nervous system. Myelin increases conduction speed and efficiency of neural signals. DHA from omega-3 supplementation is essential for myelin formation and maintenance. Vitamin B12 is also required for myelin integrity.
Protocol role: DHA (from krill + omega-3 top-up) and B12 (sublingual) are both in the protocol partly for myelin support. Well-myelinated circuits conduct more efficiently and are more stable — relevant to the new neural pathways being built through CC.

A precursor to glutathione (the body's primary antioxidant) and a glutamate modulator. Relevant to the rumination and compulsive aspects of the anticipatory layer.

NAD+ (Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide)Concept
A coenzyme found in every living cell, essential for cellular energy production, DNA repair, and sirtuin activation. NAD+ levels decline with age, impairing the cellular machinery neuroplasticity and longevity depend on.
Protocol role: Supported by NMN (the direct precursor). The NAD+ blood level test at Phase 0 establishes baseline and calibrates NMN dosing. Resveratrol activates sirtuins, which are the NAD+-dependent proteins NMN is designed to support.
Neural Repatterning (Technique 1)Technique
The primary CC technique. Entry conditions: SUDs 0–2, body tension below 4/10, resonance frequency breathing to baseline. Play trigger sound at low volume while simultaneously activating a genuinely felt positive emotion. Hold 3–5 minutes per repetition, 4–6 reps per session.
Protocol role: Phase 2 onward. The core rewriting mechanism. Nuropod tVNS runs throughout. tDCS (ActivaDose) applied 20 min before in Phase 3+.
Neuroplasticity WindowConcept
The period of heightened synaptic malleability following a plasticity-opening event — psilocybin macrodose, MDMA session, or SGB. During this window, new associations write more deeply and old ones are more susceptible to modification.
Protocol role: The protocol sequences CC sessions and other rewriting work to occur within active neuroplasticity windows. The post-psilocybin window runs approximately 30 days; post-SGB windows vary by individual.
Neurotransmitter SynthesisConcept
The cellular process of producing neurotransmitters (serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine, GABA, etc.) from dietary precursor amino acids and cofactors. Key cofactors include B6 (pyridoxine), B12, methylfolate, magnesium, and zinc — all present in the protocol's supplement stack.
Protocol role: The methylated B-complex and B12 in this protocol are included specifically because they are essential cofactors for neurotransmitter synthesis. On a plant-forward diet, B12 deficiency is a common and silent cause of impaired neurotransmitter production.
NGF — Nerve Growth FactorConcept
A protein involved in the survival and maintenance of sympathetic and sensory neurons. In misophonia, overactive NGF signalling in the stellate ganglion drives chronic sympathetic hyperactivation.
Protocol role: The SGB mechanism: bupivacaine temporarily interrupts NGF signalling, allowing sympathetic tone to reset. Lion's Mane stimulates NGF in the brain — a different and complementary application of the same protein.
Niacin Flush (Nicotinic Acid)Supplement
Vitamin B3 in the flush-producing form. In the Stamets Stack (psilocybin + Lion's Mane + niacin), niacin is proposed to enhance peripheral neural distribution of psilocin via vasodilation. Must produce a flush — niacinamide (non-flush) does not have the same effect.
Protocol role: Phase 3 onward. 100–200mg at 10am on Mon–Thu microdosing days, taken with psilocybin.
NMDA ReceptorConcept
N-methyl-D-aspartate receptor — a type of glutamate receptor that plays a central role in synaptic plasticity, learning, and memory consolidation. NMDA receptors are the molecular basis of "Hebbian learning" — neurons that fire together wire together. Magnesium ions block NMDA receptors at rest; this block is released during activation.
Protocol role: Both magnesium forms in the sleep stack (magnesium glycinate and magnesium L-threonate) are relevant here — magnesium modulates NMDA receptor activity. Optimal magnesium levels support the dynamic regulation of NMDA receptors that synaptic plasticity depends on.
NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)Supplement
A direct NAD+ precursor that raises cellular NAD+ levels. Essential for DNA repair, mitochondrial function, and sirtuin activation.
Protocol role: Phase 1 Month 2 onward. 500mg sublingual before other supplements on empty stomach. CGN NMN Powder is the current form — placed directly under tongue.
NorepinephrineConcept
A neurotransmitter and hormone (also called noradrenaline) involved in alertness, focus, and the stress response. The locus coeruleus is the primary norepinephrine-producing brain region. tVNS activates the vagal-locus coeruleus-norepinephrine pathway to enhance plasticity during CC.
Protocol role: tVNS during CC sessions works partly by increasing norepinephrine release from the locus coeruleus, which enhances the durability of new associative memories. The cold plunge also produces a sharp norepinephrine spike.
Nrf2 PathwayConcept
Nuclear factor erythroid 2–related factor 2 — a transcription factor that activates the body's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory gene expression programs. When activated, Nrf2 upregulates hundreds of cytoprotective genes simultaneously. Sulforaphane (from broccoli/brassicas) is one of the most potent known Nrf2 activators.
Protocol role: Activated by sulforaphane (brassicas, prepared correctly), quercetin (capers), and exercise. The Nrf2 pathway explains why whole-food dietary interventions produce effects that far exceed what isolated antioxidant supplements achieve — it activates the body's endogenous antioxidant production rather than providing antioxidants exogenously.
Nuropod (tVNS)Device
A transcutaneous vagus nerve stimulation device. An electrode clip delivers gentle electrical stimulation to the cymba concha of the left ear, activating the auricular branch of the vagus nerve. Produces parasympathetic upregulation and enhanced norepinephrine-pathway plasticity during CC.
Protocol role: Phase 2 onward. Clipped 2–3 minutes before CC session begins, runs throughout. Settings: 25Hz, 200–300 µs pulse width, 0.5–1.0 mA — below sensation threshold. Also used as standalone support during high-trigger periods.

OxytocinConcept

A type of fat molecule that forms the structural basis of cell membranes. EPA and DHA in phospholipid form (as in krill oil) incorporate directly into neuronal membranes more efficiently than triglyceride forms.

PolyphenolConcept
A broad category of plant-derived compounds characterised by multiple phenol ring structures. Includes flavonoids (quercetin, fisetin, EGCG, anthocyanins, flavanols), stilbenoids (resveratrol), and many others. Polyphenols are generally anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and supportive of gut microbiome diversity.
Protocol role: The entire food section of this protocol is essentially a polyphenol-rich eating pattern. Capers (quercetin), berries (anthocyanins), green tea/matcha (catechins/EGCG), dark chocolate (flavanols), strawberries (fisetin), walnuts (ellagitannins), and brassicas (sulforaphane) all deliver distinct polyphenol families.
Polyvagal TheoryConcept
Stephen Porges's framework describing how the autonomic nervous system uses neural circuits — particularly the vagal pathways — to shift between safety (ventral vagal), mobilisation (sympathetic), and shutdown (dorsal vagal) states.
Protocol role: The theoretical basis for SSP, HeartMath, and tVNS. The stapedius muscle and middle ear are part of the ventral vagal circuit — SSP directly trains this pathway.
PropranololSubstance
A non-selective beta-blocker that blocks adrenaline and noradrenaline at beta-adrenergic receptors. Originally a cardiac medication. In memory reconsolidation research, it is used during the reconsolidation window to prevent the fear/stress component of a memory from being re-stored at full intensity.
Protocol role: Phase 3, weeks 4–6. 10–20mg taken 60–90 min before a CC session targeting a specific trigger memory. Used 4–6 times on SUDs 5–7 material. Requires a prescription. The factual content of the memory is preserved — only the emotional charge diminishes.
Propranolol-Assisted Memory ReconsolidationTechnique
A beta-blocker taken 60–90 min before a CC session. A specific trigger memory (SUDs 5–7) is deliberately activated to open its reconsolidation window. The propranolol prevents the stress response from being restamped at full intensity during reconsolidation.
Protocol role: Phase 3 weeks 4–6. 4–6 sessions on target memories. Requires a prescription. Work only SUDs 5–7 material. The memory's factual content is preserved — only the emotional charge diminishes.
PsilocybinSubstance
A prodrug converted to psilocin in the body. Psilocin is a serotonin 5-HT2A receptor agonist that temporarily suppresses the default mode network, increases neuroplasticity (BDNF, synaptogenesis), and enables access to emotional material that is normally defended against.
Protocol role: Session 0 (orientation), Sessions 1–3 (therapeutic, Phase 2–4), and microdosing (Phase 3+, Stamets Stack). Schedule I in the US. Macrodose sessions (2.0–3.5g) open the primary neuroplasticity windows. Microdoses (0.10–0.15g) maintain plasticity baseline between macrodoses.
Psilocybin (Microdose)Supplement
Psilocybin at sub-perceptual doses (0.10–0.15g). In the Stamets Stack, maintains neuroplasticity baseline between macrodoses.
Protocol role: Phase 3 onward. Mon–Thu at 10am with niacin flush. Requires a 0.001g precision scale.
PsoasConcept
A deep hip flexor muscle connecting the lumbar spine to the femur. Often called the "fight-or-flight muscle" because it contracts during the defensive startle response and during chronic psychological stress. Chronic psoas tension signals ongoing danger to the brainstem, maintaining sympathetic activation.
Protocol role: The psoas release in the full somatic release sequence (Phase 3 onward) is one of the most important elements. Its direct connection to the diaphragm means psoas tension compresses breathing and perpetuates the physiological stress response. Releasing the psoas is among the most direct physical interventions for lowering the sympathetic floor.

QuercetinConcept

Reconsolidation WindowConcept
The brief period (roughly 1–6 hours) after a memory is retrieved during which it is malleable and can be modified before being re-stored. Requires the memory to be actively destabilised — mere recall without emotional activation is insufficient.
Protocol role: Exploited by the propranolol protocol: specific trigger memories are activated vividly, then propranolol prevents full re-stamping of the threat response during reconsolidation.

ResveratrolSupplement

RuminationConcept
Repetitive, passive focus on distressing thoughts, feelings, or experiences without moving toward active problem-solving or acceptance. In misophonia, rumination about triggers — replaying incidents, anticipating future ones, constructing narratives of violation — maintains and amplifies the threat circuit between actual trigger events.
Protocol role: The anti-rumination protocol (Section 6E) addresses this directly. The ACT defusion technique and time-container approach are the primary tools. NAC's glutamate-modulating mechanism is specifically relevant to the compulsive quality of rumination loops.

An auditory intervention. Music is acoustically filtered to enhance the human vocal frequency range. This exercises the stapedius muscle in the middle ear (part of the ventral vagal circuit), improving the neural system that distinguishes safety signals from threat signals.

Protocol role: Phase 0 baseline at Base Clinic Akasaka, Tokyo (¥44,000). Repeat at Phase 4 if logistics allow. Tracks the biological impact of the protocol on HPA axis regulation.
SenolyticsConcept
Compounds that selectively clear senescent ("zombie") cells — cells that have stopped dividing but remain metabolically active and secrete inflammatory signals. Senescent cell accumulation drives chronic inflammation and accelerated aging.
Protocol role: Dietary senolytics in the protocol: fisetin (strawberries), quercetin (capers), and anthocyanins (berries). These are emphasised in Phase 5 as part of the cellular wellness layer.
Sensorimotor CortexConcept
The region of the brain (sitting at the boundary of motor and sensory cortices) that processes both sensory information and the motor commands associated with it. In misophonia, the sensorimotor cortex corresponding to the body part making the trigger sound activates when the sound is heard — a mirror-neuron-like simulation.
Protocol role: Identified in Kumar et al. (2021) as the initiating region in the misophonic cascade. The sensorimotor activation (simulation of the eating/breathing action) precedes and drives the subsequent emotional and autonomic response. CC gradually decouples this sensorimotor simulation from its threat associations.
SerotoninConcept
A neurotransmitter involved in mood regulation, social behaviour, and the sleep-wake cycle. Psilocybin works primarily via serotonin 5-HT2A receptor agonism. MDMA causes massive serotonin release, which accounts for much of its therapeutic effect on the interpersonal layer.
Protocol role: Post-MDMA: serotonin stores are depleted and need 3 days of 5-HTP + EGCG to replenish. Do not take 5-HTP or tryptophan in 24 hours before any psilocybin or MDMA session — the excess serotonin precursor loading may blunt or dangerously amplify effects.
SGB — Stellate Ganglion BlockConcept
An injection of local anaesthetic (bupivacaine) into the stellate ganglion — a sympathetic nerve cluster in the neck. Temporarily interrupts overactivated NGF signalling, producing a lasting reset of the sympathetic floor.
Protocol role: Phase 0 structural intervention and Phase 4 opening reset. DSR bilateral protocol: right side first, left 24–72 hours later. Ultrasound guidance non-negotiable. Effect develops over 1–4 weeks.
SirtuinConcept
A family of proteins (SIRT1–SIRT7) that regulate cellular aging, DNA repair, and metabolic efficiency. Sirtuins are activated by NAD+ and caloric restriction. Resveratrol activates SIRT1 specifically.
Protocol role: The NMN + resveratrol combination in Phase 5 (NMN raises NAD+, resveratrol activates sirtuins) is a synergistic longevity pairing. Both are part of the cellular wellness layer added when the intensive rewiring period is complete.
Social Threat CodingConcept
The neural process by which a trigger sound becomes associated with a specific person — encoding not just the sound but the sense of violation by that person. Activates simultaneously with the acoustic-sensory response.
Protocol role: Layer 2 of misophonic reactivity. Primary tools: MDMA-assisted processing, EMDR, IFS. Harder to address through CC alone.
Somatic BracingConcept
The pattern of chronic defensive muscle tension maintained by the nervous system in response to ongoing perceived threat — elevated shoulders, jaw clenching, contracted psoas, shallow chest breathing. This muscular tension signals ongoing danger to the brainstem, creating a feedback loop that maintains sympathetic activation.
Protocol role: The somatic release protocol targets somatic bracing directly. The simplified version (Phase 1) starts with constructive rest and diaphragmatic breathing to interrupt the brainstem-muscle feedback loop. The full sequence (Phase 3) adds psoas release, hip circles, and shoulder/neck work.
Somatic Release ProtocolTechnique
A sequence of body-based practices designed to discharge chronic defensive bracing — the muscular tension patterns (elevated shoulders, jaw clench, psoas contraction, shallow breathing) that signal ongoing threat to the brainstem.
Protocol role: Non-negotiable before every CC session. Body tension at session start directly predicts session quality. Simplified version Phase 1; full sequence from Phase 3: constructive rest → psoas release → hip circles → shoulder/neck release → diaphragmatic reset.
SpermidineConcept
A polyamine compound that triggers autophagy — cellular self-cleaning. Found in highest concentrations in tempeh, wheat germ, and aged cheese. One of the most evidence-supported dietary longevity interventions.
Protocol role: Daily through diet (tempeh, wheat germ). Emphasised explicitly in Phase 5 as part of the cellular renewal layer. The protocol header lists "Spermidine" as one of the core substrate tools.

A microdosing protocol combining psilocybin (0.10–0.15g) + Lion's Mane mushroom + niacin flush, taken Mon–Thu. Proposed by mycologist Paul Stamets. The niacin-induced vasodilation may enhance peripheral distribution of psilocin to neural tissue.

Stapedius MuscleConcept
The smallest skeletal muscle in the human body, located in the middle ear. Controlled by the facial nerve (CN VII), which shares anatomical proximity with the vagus nerve. When well-regulated (via ventral vagal activation), the stapedius dampens low-frequency sounds and enhances the vocal frequency range — tuning the ear toward social communication signals rather than threat signals.
Protocol role: The physiological mechanism targeted by the Safe and Sound Protocol. Filtering music through the human vocal frequency range provides acoustic exercise for the stapedius. A well-trained stapedius reduces the salience of non-vocal ambient sounds — including many trigger sounds in the eating/breathing range.
Stellate GanglionConcept
A cluster of sympathetic nerve cells located in the neck (at C7/T1 level). Part of the autonomic nervous system's sympathetic chain. In people with chronic PTSD and hyperactivation syndromes, NGF overstimulates this ganglion, maintaining a chronically elevated sympathetic floor.
Protocol role: The anatomical target of SGB. Bupivacaine injection resets NGF signalling here. The right stellate ganglion has stronger connections to the amygdala and HPA axis; the left has stronger systemic cardiovascular influence — hence the bilateral DSR protocol.

Sublingual AbsorptionConcept
Delivery of a compound under the tongue, where it is absorbed directly into the bloodstream through the mucous membrane, bypassing first-pass liver metabolism. Produces faster onset and higher bioavailability than swallowed capsules for certain compounds.
Protocol role: Used for two supplements in this protocol: NMN (500mg powder placed under tongue for 60–90 seconds) and B12 (1000mcg methylcobalamin tablet dissolved under tongue). Both achieve meaningfully higher plasma levels via sublingual delivery than equivalent oral doses.

A 0–10 scale for rating emotional distress or trigger intensity. 0 = complete calm, 10 = maximum imaginable distress.
Protocol role: Used in every CC session (pre and post) and for weekly hierarchy re-ratings. Post-session SUDs consistently lower than pre-session is the primary Phase 2 sign of working.
SulforaphaneConcept
An isothiocyanate compound produced when glucoraphanin in brassica vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower, kale) is converted by the myrosinase enzyme. One of the most potent known activators of the Nrf2 pathway. Heat deactivates myrosinase, preventing conversion — the 40-minute pre-cooking chop wait allows enzymatic conversion before heat exposure.
Protocol role: Provided by correctly prepared brassicas (chop, wait 40 min, then cook). Adding mustard powder when cooking restores myrosinase activity that heat would otherwise eliminate. Sulforaphane's Nrf2 activation produces a broad-spectrum antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effect lasting 2–3 days from a single dose.
Sympathetic FloorConcept
The resting baseline level of sympathetic nervous system activation. A high floor means every trigger fires from a higher starting point — the system is pre-loaded. Lowering the floor changes how far any given trigger can go.
Protocol role: The primary target of Phase 0 interventions (SGB, Session 0 psilocybin). The most important structural precondition for all subsequent behavioural work.
Synaptic PlasticityConcept
The capacity of synapses (the junctions between neurons) to strengthen or weaken in response to changes in activity. The molecular basis of learning and memory. Long-term potentiation (LTP) — synaptic strengthening — is how CC builds new competing associations. BDNF, magnesium, omega-3 DHA, and psilocybin all enhance synaptic plasticity.
Protocol role: The ultimate target of the entire neuroplasticity substrate layer. Every supplement, food, device, and intervention in the protocol either (1) opens the plasticity window, (2) directly strengthens synaptic plasticity, or (3) consolidates the changes that plasticity produces.

Thalamic Reticular NucleusConcept
A thin shell of inhibitory neurons that wraps around the thalamus and acts as a gain-control circuit for sensory input. When hyperactivated, it amplifies incoming sounds before they reach the cortex. When well-regulated, it filters incoming sensory data and prevents overload.
Protocol role: In chronic misophonia with habitual earplug use, the thalamic reticular nucleus increases its gain to search for sound (sensory gating amplification) — making the next trigger arrival more intense. The pink noise strategy (via bone conduction headphones) occupies this circuit to prevent gain increase. Yoga Nidra open awareness practice directly trains the thalamic reticular nucleus.

Theta WavesConcept
Brainwaves at 4–8 Hz — associated with deep relaxation, drowsiness, and hypnagogic states just above sleep onset. The theta state is where thalamic filtering is active while the cortex is in low arousal. New learning and emotional processing are enhanced in this state.
Protocol role: Yoga Nidra produces sustained theta-alpha boundary states. This is mechanistically why the open awareness phase of Yoga Nidra is the most direct thalamic filter training available in the protocol.
Theta-Alpha StateConcept
The brainwave boundary state at 4–12 Hz — the overlap between theta (deep relaxation, 4–8 Hz) and alpha (relaxed alertness, 8–12 Hz). In this state, the thalamus remains active as a sensory filter while cortical threat-processing is substantially suppressed. The state targeted by Yoga Nidra.
Protocol role: The specific target of Yoga Nidra training. Sounds experienced in the theta-alpha state register and pass without the full cortical threat cascade engaging. Sustained practice in this state is the most direct form of thalamic filter training available in the protocol — the thalamus learns to handle incoming stimuli (including trigger sounds) without routing them to threat-processing circuits.

Protocol role: Built in Phase 1 Week 4. Re-rated every Sunday throughout all phases. Downward drift over weeks without direct work is the earliest sign the substrate work is succeeding.
