Glossary

Key concepts, tools, substances, and measurements — what each is and how it fits the protocol.

A
A-MISO-SMeasurement
Amsterdam Misophonia Scale — Severity. A 6-item self-report questionnaire that measures misophonia severity on a scale of 0–24. Scores: 0–4 subclinical, 5–9 mild, 10–14 moderate, 15–19 severe, 20–24 extreme.
Protocol role: Primary outcome measure. Baseline score at Phase 0 = 18 (severe range). A 25% reduction (score ~13–14) is the Phase 2 benchmark. Measured monthly. Trajectory across all phases tells the clinical story.
Anticipatory DreadConcept
Pre-exposure anxiety that activates before any trigger sound occurs, keeping the nervous system in a state of pre-activation. The third trigger layer in misophonia, distinct from the acoustic response itself.
Protocol role: Addressed by SGB, HRV biofeedback, Yoga Nidra, kava, and tVNS. Must be reduced before effective CC work can begin — CC attempted from a chronically pre-activated state works against a significant headwind.
ACT — Acceptance and Commitment TherapyTechnique
A behavioral therapy approach that uses acceptance, defusion, and values-based action to reduce the impact of difficult thoughts and feelings without requiring their elimination.
Protocol role: Begins Phase 2. Self-directed daily. Addresses the secondary suffering layer — distress about having the distress. Does not reduce the trigger response (CC does that). Makes the period before CC completes its work livable. Primary practice: defusion — prefixing trigger thoughts with "I notice I am having the thought that…"
→ Protocol section 6D
B
BDNFConcept
Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor. A protein that supports the growth, maintenance, and survival of neurons, and is a key driver of synaptic plasticity — the ability of synapses to strengthen or weaken.
Protocol role: Elevated by psilocybin, Zone 2 exercise, Lion's Mane, Lithium Orotate, and cold exposure. BDNF peaks ~2 hours post-Zone 2 aerobic exercise — this is why CC sessions are scheduled ~2 hours after returning from the waterfall. The Pulse strategy exploits the post-session BDNF elevation.
C
CC — CounterconditioningTechnique
A behavioral rewriting mechanism that works by actively pairing a conditioned aversive stimulus (trigger sound) with a genuinely felt positive emotional state, building a competing association strong enough to inhibit the threat response over time.
Protocol role: The primary behavioral rewriting mechanism. Begins Phase 2. Distinct from exposure therapy, which relies on habituation — CC writes a new competing circuit rather than attempting to extinguish the old one through repeated exposure. Requires: calm nervous system, genuine positive emotional state, neuroplasticity window, and sleep consolidation.
→ Protocol section 6A
Convergence WindowConcept
The Phase 4 period during which all protocol tools run simultaneously at full depth from a reset autonomic baseline — the single highest-leverage intervention window in the 12-month protocol.
Protocol role: Phase 4 is the convergence. What makes it different from Phase 3 is not new tools but new conditions: SGB-reset baseline, nervous system fully prepared, dedicated reduced-trigger environment, and all tools calibrated through Phase 3.
D
DefusionConcept
An ACT technique that creates psychological distance between a person and a thought by observing the thought rather than from inside it. The canonical form: prefixing a thought with "I notice I am having the thought that…"
Protocol role: Daily ambient practice from Phase 2. Used against trigger-related thoughts, anticipatory spirals, and self-criticism. Does not suppress or argue with content — creates enough distance to interrupt the automatic cascade.
DSR — Dual Sympathetic ResetSubstance / Procedure
A specific SGB protocol using bilateral injections — right stellate ganglion first, then left ganglion 24–72 hours later. Both injections produce a more complete and durable sympathetic reset than a single unilateral procedure.
Protocol role: The minimum SGB intervention in Phase 0 and Phase 4. Ultrasound guidance is non-negotiable. Effect develops over 1–4 weeks and can persist months to years.
→ Protocol section 1B
E
EMDR — Eye Movement Desensitization and ReprocessingTechnique
A trauma processing technique that uses bilateral sensory stimulation (typically alternating audio tones) while a distressing memory is held in mind, enabling the memory to reprocess with reduced emotional charge while preserving factual content.
Protocol role: Begins Phase 3. 2–3 sessions/week post-CC. Targets the interpersonal-violation layer — specific memories where the trigger is relational, not merely acoustic. Standard CC does not reach these. Self-directed EMDR appropriate for SUDs 4–7 material.
→ Protocol section 7D
F
Felt-Sense GateConcept
A phase transition criterion based on subjective felt experience rather than a calendar or checklist. A phase whose criteria are technically met but not yet genuinely felt is not ready for transition.
Protocol role: Every phase transition in the protocol is a felt-sense gate. The principle: a phase that has not been fully absorbed cannot support what the next phase asks of it. "Not just technically checkable" is the key distinction.
H
HeartMath Inner BalanceDevice
An ear clip sensor + app that measures heart rate variability in real time and provides feedback on cardiac coherence — a state of high HRV order that reflects robust vagal tone and emotional regulation capacity.
Protocol role: Begins Phase 1 Month 2. Daily 20 min morning. Protocol: 6 breaths/min (5s in, 5s out) + a genuinely felt positive emotion. The emotion is what produces coherence — breathing alone is insufficient. Permanent daily practice in Phase 5 and Year 2.
→ Protocol section 4F
HRV — Heart Rate VariabilityMeasurement
The variation in time between consecutive heartbeats. Higher HRV reflects greater parasympathetic tone, better stress recovery capacity, and more flexible autonomic regulation. Lower HRV reflects sympathetic dominance and reduced resilience.
Protocol role: Primary longitudinal physiological feedback. Tracked from Phase 1 Day 1. A rising HRV trend over months confirms the substrate work is succeeding. Weekly trend (not daily) is the meaningful metric.
I
IFS — Internal Family SystemsTechnique
A psychotherapy model that treats the mind as a system of distinct sub-personalities or "parts," each with their own roles and motivations. Therapeutic change occurs through developing a curious, compassionate relationship with parts rather than suppressing them.
Protocol role: Begins Phase 3. 1–2 self-directed sessions/week. Addresses the misophonic protector part — the anger, the rage — not as pathology but as a part doing what it learned to do. Meeting it with curiosity can dissolve its need to protect from the inside.
→ Protocol section 7E
K
KavaSubstance
A traditional Pacific Island plant medicine with GABAergic and kavalactone-mediated anxiolytic effects. Unlike alcohol, does not impair cognition, disrupt sleep, or produce next-day effects.
Protocol role: Begins Phase 2. Situational use (max 3×/week), 45–60 min before high-trigger community contexts or challenging CC sessions. Reduces the anticipatory-dread layer. Noble kava only — not instant, extract, or tudei. As CC progresses, kava's role naturally decreases.
→ Protocol section 6F
M
Muse S — Alpha-Theta NeurofeedbackDevice
An EEG headband that measures brainwaves in real time and provides audio feedback when they drift from a target theta-alpha state (4–12 Hz). Used for active training of the brain's resting-state regulation.
Protocol role: Begins Phase 3. Daily 20 min morning after HeartMath. Trains the same theta-alpha state that Yoga Nidra inhabits — but actively via feedback rather than passively through guidance. The return to target after drifting is the training.
→ Protocol section 7B
MDMASubstance
3,4-methylenedioxymethamphetamine. An entactogen that suppresses amygdala and insula activation while producing large oxytocin surges. Creates a state of emotional openness and reduced threat reactivity.
Protocol role: Two sessions total (Phase 3 + Phase 4). Addresses the interpersonal-violation layer — the relational dimension of trigger responses that psilocybin alone cannot reach as directly. Test with Marquis + Simon's + fentanyl strips every batch. Min 6 weeks between sessions.
→ Protocol sections 7H + 8E
N
Neural RepatterningConcept
The primary CC technique — pairing trigger sounds at low volume with a genuinely felt positive emotional state in a structured session format. Technique 1 in the CC library.
Protocol role: The main behavioral rewriting mechanism from Phase 2 onward. Dual exposure: positive music in dominant ear, trigger at low volume in non-dominant ear. ~70% attention to positive, ~30% to trigger. 4–6 reps per session. Advance criterion: 4 consecutive reps at SUDs ≤2 across multiple sessions.
→ Protocol section 6A
Neuroplasticity WindowConcept
A period of elevated synaptic plasticity — the brain's capacity to form and strengthen new connections — following certain interventions. Psilocybin opens a window lasting ~30 days; MDMA opens a shorter 3–7 day window.
Protocol role: Every macrodose and MDMA session opens a window. The Pulse governs how to use it. CC work during an open window produces more durable associations than the same work outside it. Sleep consolidates changes made during the window.
P
PsilocybinSubstance
A psychedelic compound (5-HT2A agonist) that temporarily decouples the Default Mode Network, elevates BDNF and synaptogenesis, and opens a sustained neuroplasticity window lasting approximately 30 days post-session.
Protocol role: 10 total sessions across the protocol. Phase 0: Session 0 (orientation, 1.5–2g). Phase 1: 1–2 orientation sessions. Phase 2: Session 1 (first therapeutic, 2.5g). Phase 3: 1–2 deepening sessions (2.5–3g) + microdosing begins. Phase 4: One macrodose from SGB-reset baseline. Phase 5: Every 2–3 months maintenance.
→ Protocol section 6C
Propranolol-Assisted Memory ReconsolidationTechnique
A pharmacological technique using propranolol (a beta-blocker that crosses the blood-brain barrier) taken before memory reactivation. Blocks the adrenergic component of memory re-encoding, allowing a fear memory to restabilize with reduced emotional charge.
Protocol role: Begins Phase 3 second half. Requires prescription. 10–20mg, 60–90 min before CC session. Most powerful in Phase 4 where the SGB-reset baseline makes conditions uniquely favorable. Phase 3 use is preparation.
→ Protocol section 7F
The Pulse StrategyConcept
The protocol governing integration weeks after every macrodose and MDMA session. The 72 hours post-session represent the highest-value neuroplasticity window of each cycle — BDNF elevated, DMN grip loosest, dendritic spine density at peak.
Protocol role: Day 1 = rest. Days 2–3 = maximum CC effort. Days 2–7 = daily CC with tVNS. Sleep on these nights consolidates what many ordinary nights combined would. Governs every integration week across Phases 2–5.
→ Protocol section: The Pulse
S
SGB — Stellate Ganglion BlockTechnique
An ultrasound-guided injection of local anesthetic into the stellate ganglion (a sympathetic nerve cluster in the neck) that temporarily interrupts overactivated Nerve Growth Factor signaling, producing a durable reset of the sympathetic baseline.
Protocol role: Phases 0 and 4. Not sedation — a structural reset persisting months to years. Lowers the autonomic floor before behavioral work begins. Horner's syndrome (temporary eyelid droop, pupil constriction) confirms effectiveness and resolves within hours.
→ Protocol section 1B
Somatic Release ProtocolTechnique
A structured sequence of floor-based positions and breathing practices designed to release chronic defensive muscular bracing accumulated from sustained hypervigilance.
Protocol role: Phase 1 simplified version (7–10 min morning from Week 3). Phase 3 full sequence (10–15 min morning + 20–25 min pre-CC). Body tension at session start is a strong predictor of CC quality — the pre-CC sequence is non-negotiable.

SSP — Safe and Sound ProtocolDevice / Program
An auditory intervention developed by Stephen Porges that uses music processed to emphasize the human vocal frequency range, retraining the neural circuits governing auditory safety detection and the stapedius muscle in the middle ear.
Protocol role: Phase 1 primary course (5 hours, Month 2). Phase 4 mandatory refresher (Days 7–14 after SGB stabilization). Primary course primes the system; the refresher from the SGB-reset baseline rewrites it. No standalone CC on SSP days.
→ Protocol section 4G
SUDs — Subjective Units of DistressMeasurement
A 0–10 self-rating scale for measuring emotional distress in a given moment. 0 = no distress, 10 = maximum imaginable distress.
Protocol role: Used for trigger hierarchy ratings (re-rated every Sunday), CC session entry conditions (must reach SUDs 0–2), and CC advance criteria (4 consecutive reps completing at SUDs ≤2). The trajectory of hierarchy SUDs ratings over months is a key outcome measure.
T
Tactical BrakeConcept
A protocol mechanism activated when warning signs accumulate — not a break from the protocol, but a protocol feature that prevents the work from compounding the condition it is treating.
Protocol role: Activate if 3+ warning signs present (sleep poor 3+ nights, CC backfiring, rising rumination, resentment toward protocol). Minimum 48h. All active rewiring suspended. Support layer doubled. Return when: sleep stable 2+ nights, first session back at SUDs 1–3.
→ Protocol section 3C
tDCS — Transcranial Direct Current StimulationDevice
A non-invasive brain stimulation technique that uses a low-level direct current applied to the scalp to modulate neuronal excitability in targeted brain regions.
Protocol role: Begins Phase 3. 20 min before each CC session, anodal electrode at F3 (left dlPFC). Upregulates excitability of the region governing top-down emotional regulation — stronger left dlPFC → faster fear extinction. Not within 5h of sleep. Not within 24h of macrodose or MDMA.
→ Protocol section 7A
Trigger HierarchyConcept
A structured mapping of all known trigger sounds rated by SUDs level (0–10), along with their context sensitivity (who, proximity, relationship) and anticipatory component (dread before the sound).
Protocol role: Built during Phase 1 Week 4. Re-rated every Sunday throughout all phases. Not worked in Phase 1 — only mapped. CC begins at the lowest-hierarchy levels in Phase 2 and progresses upward. Hierarchy ratings shift independently of direct work as the substrate improves.
tVNS — Transcutaneous Vagal Nerve StimulationDevice
Non-invasive vagal nerve stimulation via an earclip applied to the cymba concha of the left ear, activating the auricular branch of the vagus nerve.
Protocol role: Begins Phase 2. Device: Nurosym. Used during all standalone CC sessions and macrodose CC windows. Produces parasympathetic upregulation and enhanced plasticity via the vagal-locus coeruleus-norepinephrine pathway. New CC associations form more durably when tVNS runs simultaneously.
→ Protocol section 6B
Y
Yoga NidraTechnique
A guided meditation practice that leads the practitioner into the theta-alpha boundary state (4–12 Hz) — the hypnagogic edge between waking and sleep, where the thalamus remains active as a sensory filter while the cortex enters low arousal.
Protocol role: Every evening before sleep. Never skipped. Used in this protocol as thalamic filter training — sounds register in the open awareness phase and pass without the full cortical threat cascade engaging. Ph1–2: 20 min. Ph3: 30–35 min. Ph4+: 40–45 min. Tracks must include an open awareness of external sounds phase.
