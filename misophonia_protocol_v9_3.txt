SECTION 0 — FOUNDATION

0A. The Condition and the Urgency
──────────────────────────────────

The condition is extensive. Its impact on daily life — on shared
spaces, shared meals, and the ability to be fully present with
others — is significant enough that standard behavioral approaches
have a real ceiling. The nervous system is operating at a
chronically elevated baseline that predates any given trigger event.
Every therapeutic intervention attempted from that baseline is
working against a headwind.

The protocol therefore does not begin with behavioral work. It
begins with structure: first, lower the floor. Then rewrite what
runs on it.


0B. What This Protocol Is
──────────────────────────

This is a personal healing and wellness protocol built for one
specific person, addressing one specific problem: misophonia. It
is the product of an extended inquiry into what neuroscience,
behavioral research, plant medicine, and evidence-based nutrition
each have to offer a condition for which conventional medicine has
no established treatment.

The protocol integrates multiple domains — neuroplasticity science,
psychedelic-assisted therapy, behavioral conditioning, nutrition,
exercise physiology, and somatic work — not because complexity is
a virtue, but because misophonia is a multi-layered condition that
genuinely requires tools working at different levels simultaneously.
Each element has a specific mechanistic reason to be here. Each
earns its place.

The primary goal is clear: durable, lasting reduction in misophonic
reactivity — sufficient to no longer meaningfully impair daily
life, relationships, or the ability to share space with others.
The wellness work woven throughout — the nutrition, the sleep
architecture, the cellular health — is not a separate agenda. It
builds and sustains the same biological substrate that
neuroplasticity runs on. The brain that rewires most effectively
is a brain that is well-nourished, well-rested, and operating from
a calm physiological baseline. These things are inseparable.

Success is measured by the A-MISO-S questionnaire, SUDs trajectory,
and — most importantly — behavioral change in the avoidance
inventory: what situations are you no longer avoiding? The wellness
dimension is tracked through HRV trajectory, bloodwork panels,
and subjective vitality over time.

This protocol is a living document. It establishes a starting point
and a structure — not a rigid prescription. As the work progresses,
individual elements will be adjusted based on felt sense, observed
effects, bloodwork, and what the nervous system is actually
responding to. Some things will work better than expected and
deserve more emphasis. Some will need modification. Some may be
deprioritized entirely. That is not protocol failure — it is
protocol intelligence. The structure here is the best current map.
The territory, as always, takes precedence.


0C. How Misophonia Works and Where This Protocol Intervenes
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

THE CORE CIRCUIT

When the misophonic brain hears a trigger sound, it involuntarily
activates the sensorimotor cortex corresponding to the body part
producing that sound — a mirror neuron-like process where the brain
simulates the action. That motor simulation cascades into the
anterior insula (threat signal), the anterior cingulate cortex
(amplification), and the autonomic nervous system (fight-or-flight
response). This fires faster than conscious regulation can intercept
it.

When the trigger is associated with a specific person, a second
layer activates simultaneously: social threat coding — the sense
that the triggering person is doing something to you. And running
ahead of everything is anticipatory dread: the system pre-activates
before any sound occurs, keeping the cascade perpetually primed.

Three layers. Three different neurological substrates. Three
different sets of tools.

THE THREE TRIGGER LAYERS

Layer 1 — Acoustic-Sensory
The raw conditioned response to the sound itself.
Primary tools: counterconditioning, psilocybin, tVNS, SSP.

Layer 2 — Interpersonal-Violation
Social threat coding attached to specific people.
Primary tools: MDMA-assisted processing, EMDR, IFS.

Layer 3 — Anticipatory-Dread
Pre-exposure anxiety that keeps the system chronically
pre-activated.
Primary tools: SGB, HRV biofeedback, Yoga Nidra, somatic
release, tVNS, kava.

The anticipatory layer is addressed first. Without reducing chronic
pre-activation, the acoustic layer cannot be worked effectively,
and the interpersonal layer cannot be safely approached at depth.

WHY STANDARD BEHAVIORAL THERAPY HAS A CEILING

The misophonic threat response fires because of a conditioned
circuit that activates faster than top-down regulation can reach
it. Passive exposure to that cascade does not correct it — in some
cases it deepens it. Counterconditioning is the operative
mechanism: actively pairing the trigger with a genuinely felt
positive emotional state, building a competing association strong
enough to inhibit the threat response. This requires a calm nervous
system, genuine felt positive emotion, sufficient neuroplasticity
for the new association to write durably, and sleep to consolidate
it overnight. This is what the protocol is designed to produce —
those four conditions, simultaneously, repeatedly, over a year.


0D. The Four Pillars and Governing Principles
───────────────────────────────────────────────

THE FOUR PILLARS

Pillar 1 — Plasticity Opening
Destabilizes the rigid misophonic circuit. Makes synapses malleable.
Creates conditions in which new learning writes more deeply.
Tools: Psilocybin, MDMA, Stellate Ganglion Block, microdosing.

Pillar 2 — Behavioral Rewriting
Writes new competing associations while the circuit is open.
This is the actual rewiring. Without this pillar, an open window
closes on empty.
Tools: Counterconditioning (six techniques), EMDR, propranolol-
assisted reconsolidation, IFS, ACT, cognitive reappraisal.

Pillar 3 — Consolidation
Cements structural changes during sleep. Sustains the vagal tone
and prefrontal strength that makes rewiring hold.
Tools: tVNS, tDCS, HRV biofeedback (HeartMath), Safe and Sound
Protocol, Yoga Nidra, sleep architecture.

Pillar 4 — Substrate
Sustains the biological conditions neuroplasticity runs on:
BDNF, NAD+, cellular repair, reduced baseline arousal.
Tools: Exercise, fasting, nutrition, supplementation, somatic
release, sleep hygiene, kava.

THE GOVERNING PRINCIPLES

The autonomic baseline determines the ceiling of all other work.
Until the floor is lowered, every intervention is limited by it.
This is why structural autonomic intervention comes first.

Counterconditioning is the primary rewriting mechanism. But
treatment here means two things simultaneously: writing new
associations into the circuit, and stopping daily life from
continuously re-stamping the old ones. Both are required. An
open plasticity window that closes on an unrewritten circuit
achieves little. A circuit being rewritten while the environment
keeps reinforcing the original pattern gains less than it should.
The autonomic floor, the behavioral work, and the environmental
containment are a single integrated effort.

The substrate determines how much sticks. Sleep consolidates every
structural change. Neglect the substrate and gains erode between
sessions.

The protocol must never replicate the condition it is treating.
If the protocol ever starts to feel like a burden, that is a
signal to reduce intensity — not push through. See Section 3.

Flexibility is built in, not bolted on. The structure serves
the nervous system. Not the other way around.

SECTION 1 — THE PRE-PROTOCOL RUNWAY
================================================================================

The protocol does not begin on Day 1 of Phase 1. It begins before
that — with two interventions that change what Phase 1 inherits.
Phase 1 is not starting at zero. It is continuing something already

1A. Session 0 — Orientation Psilocybin
────────────────────────────────────────

Dose: 1.5–2.0g
Timing: approximately 1 week before the Phase 0 travel window
Start time: late morning preferred

Setting: private, familiar, no risk of unexpected interruption.
A meaningful natural setting is optimal. A known indoor space
works equally well. Safe and unhurried.

Sitter: a trusted, calm presence is recommended as a witness —
not a guide or facilitator. Solo is acceptable if that allows
more genuine openness. Decide based on what will produce the
most open arrival, not obligation.

Intention: none. Arrive openly. Let the experience be whatever
it is.

This is not a therapeutic session. No CC window, no misophonia
agenda, no protocol objectives. The sole purpose is familiarity
— learning how this substance feels in your particular nervous
system before it is ever asked to work.

What Session 0 opens: a 30-day neuroplasticity window. BDNF
elevates. The default mode network begins loosening its grip on
rigid patterns. This window will still be active through the Phase
0 travel window and into the early weeks of Phase 1.

The combination with Phase 0 SGB is not engineered — it is an
organic convergence. Psilocybin opens plasticity from above; SGB
lowers the floor from below. Both in motion simultaneously, even
at low intensity, is a meaningful beginning.

Integration (Days 1–3): rest. Journal if the impulse arises.
Light exercise from Day 2. Full supplement stack resumes
immediately. No CC, no hierarchy work. Microdosing does not begin
yet.


1B. Phase 0 — Structural Intervention and Baseline Testing
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Two things need to happen before Phase 1 begins. One structural,
one diagnostic. Specific logistics — timing, location, providers
— are managed outside this document.

THE STRUCTURAL INTERVENTION — DSR SGB

A bilateral Stellate Ganglion Block using the Dual Sympathetic
Reset (DSR) protocol. Right stellate ganglion first, left stellate
ganglion 24–72 hours later.

The right side suppresses amygdala reactivity, HPA axis overdrive,
and sympathetic hyperactivation — the primary targets for
misophonia's hypervigilance profile. The left side restores
systemic autonomic balance and parasympathetic coordination.
Together: a bilateral reset of the sympathetic baseline.

Provider requirements: anesthesiologist or pain physician
experienced in SGB. Ultrasound guidance non-negotiable. Disclose
all supplements and substances in advance.

What SGB does: lowers the sympathetic floor through temporary
interruption of overactivated NGF signaling in the stellate
ganglion. Not sedation, not suppression — a durable reset that
can persist for months to years. The effect develops over 1–4
weeks.

Horner's syndrome — temporary drooping of the eyelid, pupil
constriction, and mild flushing on the injected side — is a
normal and expected post-procedure finding. It confirms the
block reached the stellate ganglion effectively. It resolves
within hours. If it does not appear, the block may have been
suboptimal — worth noting for the provider.

Post-SGB (Days 1–3): rest Day 1. From Day 2, notice ambient life:
what is resting body tension when no trigger is present? When a
trigger occurs, does it start from a lower place? How quickly does
the system recover? These observations calibrate Phase 1.

PRE-SGB SUPPLEMENT AND SUBSTANCE PROTOCOL

Pause 1 week before any SGB session:
— Krill oil
— Fish oil or algae oil (triglyceride top-up)
— NAC
— Vitamin E (if supplementing)
— Resveratrol (Phase 5 only)
All of the above resume 48 hours after the procedure.

Pause 7 days before:
— Microdosing (psilocybin)

Space at least 72 hours between a psilocybin macrodose session
and any SGB procedure. An active neuroplasticity window from a
recent macrodose is not a contraindication — the protocol
deliberately exploits this overlap. The 72-hour spacing simply
ensures you are not going into a medical procedure while still
in an altered or integrating state.

Continue normally:
— Magnesium (all forms), Lion's Mane, Bacopa, Creatine,
  NMN, B-complex, B12, D3/K2, Ashwagandha, Lithium Orotate,
  Phosphatidylserine, L. reuteri

Stop alcohol 7 days before.

Prepare your post-SGB CC session plan before the procedure.
The week after SGB is not the time to improvise.

THE DIAGNOSTIC BASELINE

Three categories of baseline data before Phase 1 begins:

Comprehensive bloodwork: metabolic panel, CBC, lipids with ApoB,
full thyroid with TPO antibodies, hormones (testosterone, DHEA-S,
cortisol), inflammation markers (hsCRP, homocysteine),
micronutrients (vitamin D, B12, ferritin, zinc, magnesium RBC),
blood glucose markers (fasting glucose, HbA1c, fasting insulin),
IGF-1.

HPA axis profile: salivary cortisol panel — diurnal rhythm across
four time points. The pre-protocol stress hormone baseline.

Cellular NAD+ level: actual cellular NAD+ status before
supplementation begins. Calibrates NMN dosing. Longitudinal
baseline for cellular renewal.

These numbers form the pre-protocol biological portrait, repeated
at Year 1 completion. Trajectory over time is the data that matters.

What Phase 0 hands to Phase 1: an active neuroplasticity window,
an SGB reset developing toward full effect, a complete biological
baseline, and a nervous system that has already received its first
structural intervention before behavioral work begins.


1C. The Assembly Window
────────────────────────

Before Phase 1 begins, a one-time disclosure to core community
leadership is worth making. Not an announcement, not an explanation
of the full protocol — a brief, direct statement that creates the
basic conditions for accommodation without requiring ongoing
management of others' reactions.

THE SOCIAL CONTRACT

The goal is simple: that visible protective practices — headphones
at meals, adjusting seating, stepping out occasionally — do not
themselves generate a second layer of social friction or require
in-the-moment explanation. At RB, part of the burden is not just
the sound but the meaning of visibly managing it around other
people. One conversation removes that secondary stressor.

Suggested framing:

"I'm working through a sound sensitivity condition that
significantly affects me in shared spaces. I'm doing structured
work on it and expect it to improve meaningfully over the coming
year. In the meantime I'll sometimes be wearing headphones at
meals or meetings, adjusting where I sit, or stepping out
briefly. I wanted you to know this is deliberate and health-
related, not social. I don't need accommodation — just awareness."

One conversation. Done. This is not a request for permission or
understanding. It is information that removes a potential secondary
stressor from the daily environment before Phase 1 begins.

ASSEMBLY CHECKLIST

Between Phase 0 and Phase 1, everything gets installed. Phase 1
should begin with the following already in place:

□ Full Phase 1 supplement stack ordered and on hand
□ Sleep protocol running: blackout mask, pink noise, sleep stack
□ Mouth taping trialed — see Section 4C
□ Yoga Nidra nightly — 20-minute tracks established
□ Morning practice progression: somatic release begins Week 3,
  Qi-Gong begins Week 4
□ Bone conduction headphones received and calibrated
□ HeartMath Inner Balance in hand
□ HRV tracking method selected and running
□ High-fidelity earplugs at every community event
□ Trigger hierarchy mapped — not worked, just rated
□ SSP primary course booked
□ Social contract disclosure made
□ Session 0 integrated and logged

When these are in place, Phase 1 has begun.

SECTION 2 — THE ARC
================================================================================

The protocol unfolds across five phases. Each phase builds on what
the previous phase established. The tools available in later phases
are more numerous not because the work is more important, but
because earlier phases built them. Nothing in the arc is a
destination. Each phase is the right work for that moment.

Phase transitions are felt-sense gates — not calendar gates. Move
forward when the current phase feels genuinely familiar and no
longer effortful as a whole. A phase that has not been fully
absorbed cannot support what the next phase asks of it. This
applies equally to every transition.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Phase 0 — Pre-Protocol
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Session 0 psilocybin. DSR SGB. Full baseline testing. Assembly.
What this establishes: the sympathetic floor is structurally
lowered before any behavioral work begins.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Phase 1 — Establishing the Soil (estimated 8–10 weeks)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
No active trigger work. One or two orientation macrodoses.
Sleep protocol, morning practices, supplement stack, SSP,
HeartMath, meeting support. Hierarchy mapped.
What this establishes: the biological substrate, sleep
architecture, daily rhythm, and regulation capacity that every
subsequent phase depends on.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Phase 2 — First Contact (estimated 6–8 weeks)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CC begins at lowest hierarchy. First therapeutic macrodose. tVNS
during standalone CC sessions. ACT. Kava as pre-exposure tool.
What this establishes: behavioral rewriting at low stakes.
The tools learned and calibrated before higher intensity.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Phase 3 — Building Capacity (estimated 8–10 weeks)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
tDCS, EMDR, IFS, propranolol-assisted reconsolidation.
MDMA Session 1. Microdosing begins. Phase 4 logistics confirmed.
What this establishes: the complete tool set, all running and
calibrated. Nothing in Phase 4 is new — this phase ensures that.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Phase 4 — Full Stack (estimated 6–8 weeks)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
All tools at full depth simultaneously. Multiple SGB sessions at
opening. Psilocybin from SGB-reset baseline. MDMA Session 2.
Intensive CC at highest hierarchy. SSP refresher. Optimal setting:
reduced daily trigger exposure for the duration.
What this does: runs all tools simultaneously at full depth from
a reset autonomic baseline, with the nervous system fully prepared.
This is possible because Phases 1–3 built the preparation. Nothing
new is introduced.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Phase 5 — Consolidation (ongoing, adaptive)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What was written in Phase 4 is only permanent if consolidated.
Protocol lightens. Real-world re-entry by choice. Wellness-
deepening supplements enter. Year 2 rhythm designed.
What this does: makes the previous phases permanent.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Summary
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Phase 0    Pre-Protocol      Floor lowered. Baseline established.
Phase 1    The Soil          Substrate built. No trigger work.
Phase 2    First Contact     CC begins. First therapeutic macrodose.
Phase 3    Building          Full tool set acquired and running.

Every phase transition is a felt-sense gate.

SECTION 3 — DAILY OPERATING MODES
================================================================================

The protocol does not run at the same intensity every day. Three
modes are defined. On any given day you are in one of them. The
mode determines what the day looks like — not willpower.


3A. Green — Resourced
──────────────────────

Run the full current-phase rhythm. Active rewiring is available
and pursued.

Indicators: slept reasonably well, body available, no rising dread,
CC feels approachable, resting body tension below 4/10.

If a plasticity window is open — post-macrodose, post-SGB — bring
full effort. Maximum productive work happens when the window is
open and the system is resourced simultaneously.


3B. Yellow — Frayed
────────────────────

A support day. Baseline and regulation practices only. No active
trigger work.

Indicators: poor sleep, rising baseline irritability, body tension
above 5/10, dread around CC, general frayedness.

Full baseline layer: supplements, sleep protocol, meeting support.
Full support layer: Yoga Nidra, somatic release, Qi-Gong, HRV
biofeedback.
Exercise if restorative. No CC. No hierarchy work. No deliberate
trigger exposure. Eat well. Rest deliberately.

One Yellow day requires no adjustment to the overall phase.


3C. Red — Tactical Brake
─────────────────────────

When warning signs accumulate, the protocol enters the Tactical
Brake. This is not a break from the protocol. It is a protocol
feature — the mechanism that prevents the work from compounding
the condition it is treating.

Activate if three or more are present, or any single one is severe:
— Sleep poor 3+ consecutive nights despite sleep protocol
— Baseline reactivity rising week over week
— CC sessions consistently ending higher than they began
— Growing dread or avoidance around CC itself
— Rumination increasing rather than decreasing
— Body tension baseline rising rather than falling
— Resentment toward the protocol has appeared
— Overall felt sense of the protocol is oppressive

Duration: minimum 48 hours. Extend until the system settles.

Active rewiring fully suspended: no CC, no hierarchy work, no
deliberate trigger exposure, no macrodosing or MDMA, no propranolol,
no EMDR.

Support layer doubled: Yoga Nidra nightly (40+ min), somatic
release every morning, Qi-Gong daily, HRV biofeedback daily.
Exercise only if genuinely restorative. Sleep protection maximized.

Baseline layer maintained: all supplements, all sleep protections,
no changes to nutrition or fasting.

Additionally: gentle movement in nature, reduce optional
commitments, do not use this time for protocol planning or analysis.

Return when: sleep stable 2+ nights, tension and dread clearly
lower, felt sense closer to "I could do a gentle session" than
"I dread it." First session back: 20 minutes, SUDs 1–3. Confirm
the system accepts the work before returning to full intensity.

If the Brake is needed more than once in a phase, revisit the
phase gate. The current phase may not be sufficiently established.
Slow the overall pace. Pushing through warning signs produces
sensitization — and sensitization undoes previous gains.

SECTION 4 — PHASE 1: ESTABLISHING THE SOIL
================================================================================

Estimated duration: 8–10 weeks
Active trigger work: none
Psilocybin: 1–2 orientation sessions (unstructured)
Devices introduced: bone conduction headphones, HeartMath
Supplements: core stack introduced in staggered waves

Phase 1 has one job: establish the conditions in which everything
that follows can work. The SGB effect from Phase 0 continues
developing. The psilocybin window from Session 0 is still active.
The nervous system is already changing. Phase 1 is not waiting
for something to begin — it is the beginning.

Introduce one new practice at a time. Each element should feel
like part of the day's natural rhythm before the next is added.
A practice rushed into place and never absorbed is not support
— it is another demand.


4A. Introduction Schedule
──────────────────────────

A guide, not a rigid sequence. If something takes longer to
establish, let it.

Week 1: Meeting support (bone conduction headphones + pink noise)
from Day 1. Sleep protocol from Day 1. Core supplements begin.
HRV tracking begins.

Week 3: Simplified somatic release (10 min morning). Bacopa
added to supplement stack.

Week 4: Qi-Gong (15 min morning). Trigger hierarchy mapping begins.

Month 2: HeartMath biofeedback begins (20 min daily). NMN sublingual
added. Phosphatidylserine added. SSP primary course booked and run.

Throughout Phase 1: one or two orientation macrodose sessions —
unstructured, no therapeutic agenda.


4B. Meeting Support
────────────────────

Begins: Week 1, Day 1
Device: bone conduction headphones (Shokz OpenRun Pro 2)

When earplugs or noise cancellation create near-silence during
meetings, the brain's thalamic reticular nucleus increases its
gain to search for sound — amplifying the next trigger when it
arrives. Bone conduction headphones leave the ear canals open
while playing pink noise at barely perceptible volume through the
cheekbones. Speech is heard clearly and naturally. The pink noise
keeps the thalamic scanning reflex occupied and prevents gain
increase.

Volume: barely audible — loud enough to register, forgotten within
30 seconds.

Use at: morning meeting, leadership meeting, council meeting,
shared meals, any context where triggers are likely.

The existing earplug practice remains valid as backup. This is
protective infrastructure — it does not advance the rewiring, but
it prevents additional reinforcement of the threat response while
the rewiring builds capacity. As CC progresses, reliance on
meeting support will reduce naturally — by choice, not schedule.


4C. Sleep Protocol
───────────────────

Begins: Week 1. Runs throughout all phases.

Sleep is the consolidation mechanism. Every structural change
produced by CC, every dendritic spine grown by psilocybin, every
extinction association formed — all are cemented during sleep, or
partially dissolve without it.

Target: 7.5–8.75 hours nightly. Consistent fixed wake time.
Sleep onset target: 9:00–10:00pm.

EVENING WIND-DOWN

8:00pm    Sleep stack (see 4J)
8:15pm    Warm shower — lowers core body temperature, the primary
          physiological trigger for sleep onset
8:30pm    Yoga Nidra — 20-minute sleep track standard nights;
          35–45-minute open-awareness track when intensive work
          is running
9:00pm    Screens off
9:30pm    In bed: pink noise on, blackout mask on

MOUTH TAPING — TRIAL

Worth trialing for 1–2 weeks. Apply a small strip of soft medical
paper tape (3M Micropore or equivalent) vertically over the center
of the lips. If it improves sleep depth or reduces night waking,
continue. If it produces discomfort or anxiety, discontinue — the
other sleep elements are sufficient.

Nasal breathing activates parasympathetic tone, produces nitric
oxide, and may reduce the cortisol-driven arousals associated with
night waking. Most people adapt within 2–3 nights.

BLACKOUT MASK

Any light penetrating the eyelids suppresses melatonin and reduces
sleep depth. A well-fitting blackout mask is non-negotiable on the
Hamakua Coast where ambient light is variable.

PINK NOISE MACHINE

Pink noise enhances slow-wave sleep and next-morning recall.
Mechanistically appropriate for a protocol actively writing new
associations that must consolidate overnight.

NIGHT WAKING

Do not check phone. Do not turn on light. Put on the 20-minute
sleep-specific Yoga Nidra track. This maintains the theta-alpha
state even if sleep does not return, preventing the cortisol-driven
frustration loop that raises the following day's reactive baseline.

MORNING PROTOCOL

Fixed wake time. Bright light within 10 minutes — outside preferred.
Cold finish on morning shower: 30 seconds cold water. This anchors
the diurnal cortisol rhythm — the morning spike should occur in
the morning, not at 3am.

WHAT DISRUPTS SLEEP IN THIS PROTOCOL

Psilocybin: always morning sessions so effects resolve before sleep.
MDMA: disrupts REM 1–3 nights after — expected; maintain Yoga Nidra.
tDCS: not within 5 hours of sleep time (approximately 4–4:30pm
  cutoff given a 9–9:30pm sleep target).
Skipping Yoga Nidra: the most common reason the night-waking
  pattern returns.
Late meals: final meal by 6:00pm — no exceptions.


4D. Morning Practices
──────────────────────

SOMATIC RELEASE — SIMPLIFIED VERSION (begins Week 2)

Chronic misophonic hypervigilance creates sustained defensive
bracing: shoulders elevated, jaw clenched, psoas contracted,
breath shallow. This muscular tension signals ongoing danger to

7–10 minutes total. Run every morning before the day's demands begin.

Avoid: if producing sharp pain in any position, come out and

4G. Safe and Sound Protocol — SSP
───────────────────────────────────

Begins: Month 2, Weeks 3–4
Platform: Unyte-iLs (licensed provider required)
Total: 5 hours — 30–60 minutes per session over 1–2 weeks

The SSP uses music with the human vocal frequency range
acoustically enhanced to retrain the neural circuits governing
auditory safety detection. The stapedius muscle in the middle ear,
when well-regulated, dampens low-frequency (threat-associated)
sounds and enhances the vocal (safety-associated) range. In chronic
hypervigilance the stapedius is undertrained. SSP exercises the
stapedius and its vagal circuits, operating upstream of the
conditioned trigger response — at the auditory hardware level
before the threat cascade fires.

SSP does not do what CC does. It addresses what CC cannot reach
from above. The two are complementary.

Running SSP: lie down or sit comfortably. Headphones — not
speakers. No multitasking. If emotional release, fatigue, or
hypersensitivity occurs: slow down, do not push through.

On SSP days: do not run standalone CC sessions. The auditory
system is being worked hard enough by SSP alone — adding formal
CC sessions risks overstimulation. tDCS continues as normal on SSP
days; it does not involve auditory processing and is mutually
reinforcing with the receptive state SSP requires.
The CC window inside a macrodose session (Hours 4–5) is unaffected
— it occurs within the session's own arc and is not a standalone
CC session.

Kava before an SSP session is acceptable. If anticipatory anxiety
around SSP's frequencies is present, kava may improve receptivity.
Avoid only if it produces notable sedation that reduces alert
engagement with the material.

After primary course: 2–3 days before pushing CC hierarchy.

A mandatory SSP refresher runs in Phase 4 from the SGB-reset
baseline, where it lands at greater depth than the Phase 1 primary
course could. The primary course primes the system; the refresher
rewrites it.

If provider access is unavailable: daily listening to music in the
human vocal frequency range — acoustic folk, unprocessed vocal
harmonies, classical violin — for 20–30 minutes in a mildly alert
state. Meaningfully better than nothing.

ON OMEGA-3 SOURCES — WHY KRILL OIL AND FISH

This protocol's neuroplasticity targets require sustained high
levels of EPA and DHA in phospholipid form for neuronal membrane
incorporation. DHA is the dominant fatty acid in the brain's gray
matter and myelin. During intensive neuroplasticity work, neuronal
membranes are being actively remodeled — the availability of DHA
in phospholipid form directly affects how efficiently new synaptic
connections form and stabilize.

Algae oil provides EPA and DHA in triglyceride form, which is
well absorbed and raises blood levels effectively. Krill oil
provides them in phospholipid form, which crosses into neural
tissue more efficiently and incorporates into neuronal membranes
at a higher rate. There is currently no plant-based phospholipid
EPA+DHA source available in supplement form — krill oil is the
only practical source of this specific delivery mechanism.

Oily fish (sardines, mackerel, salmon) provides EPA+DHA at
~1500–2000mg per 100g serving, predominantly in triglyceride form
but at a dose and with accompanying nutrients (vitamin D, selenium,
iodine) that are difficult to replicate from supplements alone.

The choice is yours. A triglyceride top-up alone is good. The krill
+ top-up combination (fish oil or algae oil) is better for this
specific application. The protocol states this clearly and leaves the
decision where it belongs.

SECTION 5 — COMMUNITY-LIFE CONTAINMENT DURING REWIRING
================================================================================

The formal CC session is the engine of behavioral rewriting. But
the nervous system does not distinguish between a CC session and
daily life. Every communal meal, every morning meeting, every car
ride is also a data point — either reinforcing the old circuit or
failing to add to it. The goal during active rewiring phases is
not to eliminate real-world exposure, but to ensure that real-
world encounters do not continuously re-stamp the original pattern
faster than CC is rewriting it.

This section is operational. It describes the specific contexts
at RB and how to navigate each one with minimal reinforcement of
the condition while the rewiring is underway.


5A. Meals
────────────

Pre-entry check: run the Green/Yellow/Red assessment before
entering any shared meal. On Yellow days, apply full containment
protocol. On Red days, a solo meal is not avoidance — it is
appropriate protection.

Seating: edge or corner seating with clear exit access. Back to
a wall where possible. This reduces the number of people in the
peripheral sound field and preserves the option to leave without
social friction.

Timing: arriving slightly early or slightly late to avoid the
high-density seating scramble. Once seated, position is set.

Sound support: bone conduction pink noise as default. Earplugs
as backup for high-load days. Both simultaneously is acceptable —
the goal is arriving at the table in a regulated state, not
demonstrating resilience prematurely.

Meal duration: there is no obligation to stay for the full
duration of any communal meal. A short, complete meal followed
by a clean exit is a regulated experience. Forcing a prolonged
stay when the nervous system is escalating is not practice —
it is reinforcement.

What counts as a supported meal: any meal attended with sound
support, chosen seating, and a regulated exit. Not a failure.
Not a crutch. Infrastructure for this phase.

What counts as an unnecessary exposure: attending a meal on a
Red day without any containment because it seemed like the right
thing to do. Unnecessary exposures do not advance the work. They
cost the nervous system currency it could spend elsewhere.


5B. Morning Meetings and Council
────────────────────────────────────

Pre-entry: same Green/Yellow/Red check. On Yellow, full
containment. On Red, attendance is optional — the community
will survive one absence.

Sound support: bone conduction pink noise as default from Week 1.
No adjustment or explanation required mid-meeting.

Physical position: aisle or exit-adjacent seating where the
format allows. Not front-center where scanning the room requires
turning toward trigger sources.

During the meeting: note-taking, hand position, partial attention
to materials rather than faces — all acceptable regulatory tools.
The meeting's content can be followed equally well with slightly
redirected attention.

Stepping out: brief exits require no explanation. A trip to the
bathroom, a minute outside, a brief movement break — these are
regulation tools used by many people for many reasons.

Post-meeting decompression: 3–5 minutes before transitioning to
the next demand. Physiological sigh, slow walk outside, brief
constructive rest, or simply standing in a quiet space and letting
the nervous system settle. Prevents the meeting's arousal load
from carrying directly into the next context.


5C. Car Rides
─────────────────

Risk assessment: one-on-one rides with a person whose sounds are
high on the trigger hierarchy carry significant risk — enclosed
space, sustained proximity, no exit. Group rides distribute the
risk across more people and more ambient sound.

Mitigation in high-risk rides:
— Window open, even slightly — adds ambient noise and ventilation
— Music or podcast playing as background
— Front seat when possible — slightly more distance, more visual
  field
— Bone conduction headphones are not conspicuous in a car

When declining is appropriate: if the ride is non-essential, one-
on-one, and the nervous system is already Yellow — declining is
not avoidance. Avoidance is the pattern of systematically
restructuring life to never encounter the trigger. A single
declined ride during active rewiring is a calibration decision.

When accepting with containment is better: if declining would
create more social friction than the ride itself, attending with
full containment protocol is often the lower-cost choice.


5D. Yoga and Shared Quiet Practices
───────────────────────────────────────

Yoga and meditation formats create a specific secondary problem:
structural silence heightens thalamic scanning. The ambient sounds
that background noise normally masks become prominent. This is not
a reason to avoid yoga — it is a reason to approach it
strategically.

Position: edge placement, near the door, not surrounded.

Sound: bone conduction at barely perceptible volume during setup,
then off once practice begins and the system is settled. The pink
noise at onset is settling, not performance support.

Partial attendance: arriving for movement portions and leaving
before extended savasana or silent sitting is a legitimate choice
in active rewiring phases. Full attendance in later phases when
the thalamic filter is better trained.

If escalation occurs mid-practice: leave quietly. The practice
is not a CC session. Staying through flooding in a yoga class
advances nothing.

What counts as successful participation: attended, regulated for
the portion attended, exited before significant escalation.
Not heroism. Not avoidance. Calibration.


5E. Work Coordination and One-on-One Interactions
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Short check-ins over long discussions: preference for brief, task-
focused exchanges over extended unstructured conversation with
people whose sounds are high on the trigger hierarchy.

Walking meetings: a genuine tool. Side-by-side movement reduces
the face-to-face acoustic intimacy of sitting conversations and
adds ambient environmental sound as cover.

Written communication: using text or written formats for non-urgent
exchanges is not social withdrawal — it is a practical tool during
active rewiring phases when interpersonal sound exposure needs to
be budgeted deliberately.


5F. Post-Event Reset
───────────────────────

After any high-load communal context — regardless of how well
it went — a brief reset before the next demand:

2–5 minutes. Options:
— Physiological sigh (double inhale through nose, long exhale
  through mouth) × 3
— Brief walk outside, no destination
— Constructive rest position, eyes closed
— Cold water on face and wrists
— Three slow breaths with jaw deliberately released

The reset is not optional on days the context was difficult.
It is optional on days it went well — though it helps in both.

The distinction that matters: "I got hit" (a trigger activated,
I absorbed it, I am now settling) versus "I am spiraling" (the
trigger activated and the post-event loop is running and building).
The reset interrupts both. The anti-rumination protocol in Section
6E is the next step if spiral is present after the reset.

The reset is not analysis time, not protocol review time, not a
moment to evaluate progress. It is a physiological action. Two
minutes. Then continue.

THE PULSE STRATEGY — READ BEFORE ANY SESSION PROTOCOL

Every macrodose and MDMA session in this protocol is followed by
an integration week. The most important concept governing that
week is the Pulse.

The 72 hours immediately after a session are the highest-value
neuroplasticity window in each cycle. BDNF is elevated. Dendritic
spine density is at its post-session peak. The default mode
network's grip on existing patterns is at its loosest. New
associations formed in this window are retained more durably than
those formed at any other time.

Day 1 is always rest — the system needs it. Days 2 and 3 are
maximum productive CC effort. Sleep on those nights consolidates
what many ordinary nights combined would.

The Pulse applies to every session in Phases 2–9. Whenever
integration week appears in the text, this is the operating
logic behind it. The phrase "this is the Pulse" in later
sections is a callout to return here.


6A. Counterconditioning — How It Works
────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT CC IS AND IS NOT

CC is not exposure therapy. Exposure therapy relies on habituation
— repeat until the nervous system stops reacting. The misophonic
threat circuit fires faster than top-down regulation can reach it.
Passive exposure does not correct this. It can deepen it.

CC actively pairs the trigger with a genuinely felt positive
emotional state, building a competing association strong enough
to inhibit the threat response. Two non-negotiable conditions:
1. The positive state must be physically felt in the body — not
   conceptually invoked.
2. Entry condition must be met: SUDs 0–2, body tension below 4/10.
   If this cannot be reached after 5 minutes of resonance frequency
   breathing, the session does not begin. This is a Yellow day.

PAIRED STIMULI LIBRARY — BUILD BEFORE FIRST SESSION

Music: 3–5 pieces producing genuine positive emotion — chills,
warmth, uplift. 30–40-minute playlist. Test weekly. Replace any
piece that stops producing a genuine response.

Memory anchor: one vivid, specific positive memory accessible
within 30 seconds. Specific sensory detail. Rehearse until
automatic.

Trigger sound library: recordings organized by hierarchy level
from SUDs 1–3 upward. Sources: Freesound.org, FOAMS database
(Zenodo — 32 files across 8 sound classes with pilot distress
ratings), yt-dlp for YouTube, self-recording. Organize by SUDs
level. 3–5 distinct recordings per trigger type per band —
variation prevents habituation to a single recording.

TECHNIQUE 1 — NEURAL REPATTERNING (primary technique, all phases)

Entry: SUDs 0–2, body tension below 4/10. Reached via resonance
frequency breathing — 6 breaths per minute (5-second inhale,
5-second exhale), 3–5 minutes.

Session structure (20–30 minutes, 4–6 repetitions):

Step 1 — Priming (2–3 min): positive playlist. Allow emotion to
build until genuinely felt in the body. Do not proceed until
physically present.

Step 2 — Dual exposure (2–4 min): positive music at comfortable
volume in dominant ear; trigger sound at low volume in
non-dominant ear. Positive carries ~70% of attention; trigger ~30%.
When attention is pulled to trigger, redirect gently — do not fight.

Step 3 — Recovery (1–2 min): trigger off. Positive music only.
Allow positive state to re-establish.

Step 4 — Rest (2 min): silence or ambient sound.

Repeat 4–6 times. Advancement criterion: 4 consecutive repetitions
completing at SUDs ≤2. When consistent across multiple sessions,
move one step up the hierarchy.

SCHEDULING CC SESSIONS

Phase 2 frequency: 3–4 standalone sessions per week, Green days
only.

Not on Yellow days. Not as a standalone session on macrodose days
— the macrodose session contains the CC work for that day (Hours
4–5). Day 1 after a macrodose is rest only; CC resumes Day 2.
Daily standalone CC from Days 2–7 of integration week — the most
neurologically active CC window of each cycle.

Best timing: training days — 12:00pm (in the gap between work
blocks). Aerobic days — 5:00pm (after work block 2 ends; BDNF
from the morning waterfall is still elevated).

Avoid: on Yellow days. Late evening.

6B. tVNS — Vagal Nerve Stimulation
─────────────────────────────────────

Begins: Phase 2, with first CC sessions
Device: Nuropod earclip tVNS
Use: during standalone CC sessions

Auricular tVNS activates the vagus nerve via the ear's auricular
branch, producing parasympathetic upregulation and enhanced
plasticity in the locus coeruleus via the norepinephrine pathway.
Applied during CC, new associations retain more durably than
without it.

Settings: 25Hz, 200–300 microseconds pulse width, 0.5–1.0 mA.
Begin at 0.5 mA. No sensation should be felt — any tingling or
discomfort means the setting is too high, reduce it.

Protocol: clip to left ear (cymba concha per Nuropod instructions).
Start 2–3 minutes before the CC session begins. Run throughout.
Stop with the session.

On macrodose days: tVNS during the Hours 4–5 CC window is
optional. Psilocybin opens plasticity through 5-HT2A agonism and
DMN decoupling; tVNS works through the vagal-locus coeruleus-
norepinephrine pathway. These are additive, not redundant. If the
device can be clipped and running before the CC window opens
without adding distraction to the session, use it. If managing
the device would interrupt the session's quality, omit it. Not a
rule — a judgment call made before the session begins.

Avoid: not during driving or activities requiring full alertness.

6D. ACT — Acceptance and Commitment Therapy
─────────────────────────────────────────────

Begins: Phase 2
Format: self-directed, daily and weekly
Resource: The Happiness Trap (Russ Harris)

ACT addresses the secondary suffering layer: distress about having
the distress, self-criticism for reacting, anticipatory anxiety,
and rumination between CC sessions. It does not reduce the trigger
response — CC does that. It makes the period before CC completes
its work livable.

Defusion practice (daily, 2–3 minutes): when any trigger-related
thought, self-criticism, or anticipatory worry arises, prefix it
silently: "I notice I am having the thought that..."
This creates distance between the thought and the thinker without
suppressing content. The more automatic it becomes, the more
useful it is. If it starts feeling like obligation, simplify to
the single phrase and use it lightly.

Values-based action (weekly, one instance): one action consistent
with who you want to be that requires tolerating some misophonic
discomfort. Acknowledge the discomfort before. Note afterward that
you did it. Core ACT move: not elimination of discomfort, but
action in accordance with values despite it.


6E. Anti-Rumination Protocol
──────────────────────────────

Misophonia does not only activate during live exposure. The
anticipatory and post-event loops — pre-meeting dread, post-
trigger replay, rehearsed imagining of future encounters — can
consume more nervous system bandwidth than the actual triggers.
Left unaddressed, rumination keeps the threat circuit primed
continuously, regardless of how well formal CC is progressing.

Three named practices:

PATTERN INTERRUPT — immediate
When rumination is recognized: physical break in the pattern.
Stand up, change room, cold water on face, 10-second shaking
practice, or the physiological sigh double-inhale. The content
of the rumination does not need to be addressed in the moment.
The loop needs to be broken. Break the pattern first.

ACT DEFUSION — within 60 seconds
After the pattern interrupt: apply ACT defusion directly to the
ruminating content. "I notice I am rehearsing the scenario
where..." The prefix creates distance between the loop and the
person running it. Do not argue with the content, analyze it,
or try to resolve it. Label it and let it be.

TIME CONTAINER — for genuine processing
If the loop is persistent and feels like it contains something
real — not anxiety noise but an actual unresolved relational
dynamic — give it a container: 5–10 minutes, sitting, pen and
paper, write out what is actually being processed. Then close
the notebook. The container is not permission to spiral — it is
a bounded space so the processing does not leak across the
whole day.

Note: rumination increasing rather than decreasing is a Tactical
Brake indicator. See Section 3C.


6F. Kava — Pre-Exposure Anxiolytic
────────────────────────────────────

Begins: Phase 2. Maximum 3x per week, situational.
Timing: 45–60 minutes before a high-trigger community context or
a CC session at a more challenging hierarchy level.

Traditional Pacific Islander anxiolytic with GABAergic and
kavalactone-mediated effects. Unlike alcohol, does not impair
cognition, disrupt sleep, or produce next-day effects. Reduces
the anticipatory-dread layer before high-trigger contexts. As CC
progresses and the anticipatory layer reduces, kava's role

Noble kava only — not instant, not extract, not tudei kava.
2–4 tablespoons medium-grind noble kava root powder in a strainer
bag. 2 cups room-temperature water — hot water degrades
kavalactones. Knead and squeeze 5–10 minutes until muddy brown.
Remove bag. Drink the full preparation. Effects begin 15–30 min.

Avoid: macrodose days, MDMA days, with alcohol, daily use.

7A. tDCS — Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation
────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Begins: Phase 3
Device: ActivaDose tDCS
Frequency: 3x per week (building to 5x in Phase 4)
Timing: 20 minutes immediately before each CC session

Low-level direct current applied to the scalp upregulates
excitability of the left dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (dlPFC)
— the region governing top-down emotional regulation. Stronger
left dlPFC activation correlates with faster fear extinction and
better override of automatic threat responses.

Placement:
Anodal electrode: left dlPFC — position F3 (10-20 EEG system),
above-left of center, roughly above the left eyebrow and back
2 inches.
Reference electrode: right supraorbital (above right eyebrow).

Settings: 1.5–2.0 mA. Begin at 1.5 mA. Brief tingling at onset
is normal and resolves within 60–90 seconds. Reduce to 1.0 mA
if persistent discomfort.

Protocol: apply electrodes, start device, sit quietly or review
the upcoming CC session plan for 20 minutes. Begin CC immediately
when tDCS completes — the excitability window is active for
approximately 30–60 minutes post-stimulation. tVNS then runs
during the CC session. Sequence: tDCS (20 min) → CC with tVNS.

Avoid: not within 5 hours of sleep time (approximately 4–4:30pm
cutoff given a 9–9:30pm sleep target). Not within 24 hours of
a macrodose or MDMA session. Not during Tactical Brake. Stop if
persistent headache develops.

7C. EMDR — Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Begins: Phase 3
Frequency: 2–3 sessions per week, post-CC session, on CC days
Target: specific interpersonal trigger memories — specific
incidents, specific people, specific moments. Not generic sounds.

EMDR activates a distressing memory while engaging bilateral
sensory stimulation (bilateral audio tones). The bilateral
stimulation appears to activate the brain's innate memory
processing mechanism — the same one operating during REM sleep
— allowing the memory to reprocess with reduced emotional charge.
The factual content is preserved; the threat response attached
to it diminishes.

For misophonia, EMDR targets the interpersonal-violation layer —
memories where the trigger is not just acoustic but relational.
Standard CC does not reach these memories directly. EMDR does.

Self-directed protocol: use a bilateral audio app (alternating
left-right tones at 1 Hz, e.g. Brain.fm bilateral, EMDR Therapy
app). Headphones required.

Step 1 — Target: one specific memory. Rate distress (SUDs 0–10).
Step 2 — Activation: bring memory vividly to mind — sight, sound,
physical sensation. Hold it.
Step 3 — Bilateral stimulation: start audio. Hold the memory
while tones run. ~30-second sets. Notice what shifts — images,
emotions, physical sensations, new associations. Do not direct.
Step 4 — Check-in: after each set, breath, note what came up,
re-rate distress. If reducing, run another set. If stable or
rising after 3–4 sets, close and ground.
Step 5 — Closure: when distress reaches 0–2, install a positive
cognition. Choose a brief believable opposite statement. Run
1–2 sets on this statement.
Grounding: 5 breaths, 5 things noticed in the room, feet on
floor. Do not end a session with distress above 3/10.

Working with a trained EMDR therapist is preferable to self-
directed work for high-charge memories. Self-directed EMDR is
appropriate for SUDs 4–7 material.

Avoid: same day as a macrodose or MDMA session. During Tactical
Brake. When baseline distress is already elevated. Never start
with highest-charge material — build up from lower-charge memories.


7D. IFS — Internal Family Systems
───────────────────────────────────

Begins: Phase 3
Frequency: 1–2 self-directed sessions per week, post-trigger
or post-EMDR

The misophonic rage is not a malfunction. It is a protector part
doing what it has learned to do. Suppressing it hardens it.
Meeting it with curiosity can dissolve the need for it from the
inside.

Self-directed IFS session (15–20 minutes): quiet space, eyes
closed, slow breaths. Bring to mind a recent trigger moment or
the felt sense of misophonic reactivity. Notice where it lives
in the body. Turn toward it with curiosity — not analysis.

Ask: "What are you trying to protect me from?"
Wait. Do not force. An answer will come — words, images, felt
sense.
Acknowledge: "I hear you. I understand you are trying to help."
Ask: "What are you afraid would happen if you didn't react this
way?"
Continue 15–20 minutes. Write the exchange immediately after —
as record, not analysis.

Avoid: macrodose days, MDMA days, Tactical Brake, very high
baseline distress.


7E. Propranolol-Assisted Memory Reconsolidation
─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Begins: Phase 3 second half (CC running at SUDs 5–6)
Requires: physician prescription
Frequency: 2–3x per week for 4–6 weeks during high-hierarchy
CC work

When a fear memory is actively recalled it briefly destabilizes
before restabilizing. Propranolol (beta-blocker, crosses the
blood-brain barrier), taken before memory reactivation, blocks
the adrenergic component of re-encoding. The memory restabilizes
with reduced emotional charge. Factual content preserved; fear
response diminished.

Protocol: propranolol 10–20mg, 60–90 minutes before the CC
session. Begin session by vividly activating a specific trigger
memory through imaginal exposure — this opens the
reconsolidation window. Proceed with Technique 1. Log SUDs
before and after every repetition. Work SUDs 5–7 material —
one level above normal working range.

Sequence on propranolol days: tDCS (20 min, running during the
60–90 min propranolol wait window) → CC with tVNS → EMDR post-
session if scheduled.

The most potent propranolol window in this protocol is Phase 4
— the SGB-reset baseline makes the conditions uniquely favorable.
Phase 3 use is preparation: learning the protocol and working
SUDs 5–6 material before Phase 4 asks for more.

Avoid: within 48 hours of macrodose or MDMA session. Not for
asthma or low baseline heart rate. Not during Tactical Brake.

7G. MDMA Session 1 — Interpersonal Layer
──────────────────────────────────────────

largest oxytocin surge of any known compound. For a trigger
profile that is significantly interpersonal, MDMA reaches the
layer psilocybin alone cannot access as directly. Session 1 is
modest in dose and ambition — 75mg, lower pressure. The goal is
to learn what working in this layer feels like. The learning here
makes Phase 4's Session 2 go deeper.

test strips — every batch, without exception. Do not combine with

rather than emotionally intense, introduce lowest-charge

  Serotonin replenishment — indicated for MDMA specifically,
  which depletes serotonin through excess release. Not used
  after psilocybin sessions.

Minimum interval between MDMA sessions: 6 weeks. Do not exceed
two MDMA sessions in any 12-month period.

9A. Deliberate Re-Entry
────────────────────────

what situations are you no longer avoiding?

Phase 5 answers this through deliberate re-entry — choosing to
engage with previously avoided contexts, by choice rather than
schedule or pressure. This is not exposure therapy. It is
choosing, from the protocol's new baseline, to be present and
let the nervous system demonstrate what has changed.

the lowest-charge item. Choose to enter it without the full
protective stack — fewer earplug adjustments, without kava,
without a prepared exit plan. Note what is different. Progress
up the list over weeks and months as readiness arrives.

nervous system demonstrates it no longer needs them — not when
a calendar suggests it. Some contexts may warrant continued
support indefinitely. That is not failure — it is appropriate
calibration.

SECTION 10 — REFERENCE: SOMATIC RELEASE PROTOCOL
================================================================================

PHASE 1 VERSION — SIMPLIFIED (7–10 minutes, morning only)

Begins Week 3 of Phase 1. The entry point before the full
sequence is established.

Constructive rest (5 min): lie on back, knees bent, feet flat,
hands on lower belly. Allow the lower back to release toward
the floor. Breathe into the hands. Jaw released, eyes soft.

Diaphragmatic reset (2–3 min): inhale 4 counts into the belly,
pause 2 counts, exhale 6 counts. Allow jaw and shoulders to
release on each exhale.

That is the complete simplified version. No more is needed in
Phase 1.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PHASE 3 ONWARD — FULL SEQUENCE (20–25 min before CC;
10–15 min morning only)

Constructive rest (5 min): on back, knees bent, feet flat,
hands on lower belly. Lower back releases toward floor.
Breathe into the hands. Jaw released. Eyes soft.

Psoas release (5 min): from constructive rest, allow one knee
to slowly drop outward — do not force. Hold 2 minutes, return.
Other side. The psoas connects to the diaphragm; its release
directly reduces the brainstem's danger signal.

Hip circles (3 min): standing, hands on hips. Slow circles
1 minute each direction, then figure-eights.

Shoulder and neck release (3 min): slow head semicircles forward
only. Shoulder shrugs held 3 seconds, released. Jaw drops, holds
5 seconds, releases.

Diaphragmatic reset (3 min): inhale 4 counts into belly, pause
2 counts, exhale 6 counts with audible release. Jaw drops on
each exhale.

Integration (2 min): standing, notice body, shake out hands,
one slow breath.

Avoid: do not force sharp pain. The psoas release should feel
like release, not stretch. If any position produces anxiety,
remain in constructive rest.

11A. What Yoga Nidra Is in This Protocol
──────────────────────────────────────────

Yoga Nidra is used here as thalamic filter training. In the
theta-alpha boundary state it produces (brainwaves 4–12 Hz,
just above sleep onset), the thalamus remains active as a
sensory filter while the cortex enters low arousal. Sounds
register and pass without the cortical threat cascade being
fully online.

By practicing open awareness of ambient sounds — including
trigger-range sounds — from this state, the thalamic filter
is trained directly. Sounds that would normally activate the
insula-cortex threat cascade begin to register as information
rather than threat.

Distinct from HeartMath (vagal regulation from alert state) and CC
(conscious association rewriting). Yoga Nidra trains what happens
to sensory input in the passively filtered state — and does so
through direct experience rather than external feedback, building
the intuitive awareness the other practices support.


11B. Protocol
──────────────

Timing: every evening, before sleep. Never skipped.

Track progression:
Phases 1–2: 20-minute tracks
Phase 3: 30–35-minute tracks
Phase 4 onward: 40–45-minute tracks

Track selection: must include body scan, visualization, and
open awareness of external sounds phases. The open awareness
phase is where the thalamic filter training happens — do not
use tracks that omit it.

Starting points: Jennifer Piercy "Yoga Nidra for Sleep,"
Huberman Lab NSDR, Ally Boothroyd. Test multiple tracks and
identify 2–3 that reliably produce the hypnagogic edge —
aware but deeply relaxed, body absent or heavy, time sense
altered.


11C. Open Awareness Phase
──────────────────────────

Do not redirect attention away from ambient sounds — including
sounds from community spaces. Let them register. Label
internally if helpful: "water," "voices," "movement." Allow
them to pass without bodily reaction.

If a high-trigger sound intrudes and breaks the state: note it,
return to breathing, re-enter the body scan. The recovery —
the return to stillness after disruption — is itself the
training. It is more valuable than an undisturbed session.


11D. Night Waking
──────────────────

If waking occurs: do not check phone, do not turn on light.
Put on the 20-minute sleep-specific track. This maintains
the theta-alpha state even if sleep does not return,
preventing the cortisol-driven frustration loop that raises
the following day's reactive baseline.

SECTION 12 — REFERENCE: COUNTERCONDITIONING TECHNIQUES
================================================================================

Techniques 1 and 3 are formal session techniques requiring entry
conditions: SUDs 0–2, body tension below 4/10, resonance
frequency breathing to baseline, somatic release completed.
Techniques 2, 4, 5, and 6 are ambient tools used in daily life
— no formal entry requirements apply.

TECHNIQUE 1 — NEURAL REPATTERNING (Phase 2 onward)
Primary technique throughout all phases. Full protocol in
Section 6A.

TECHNIQUE 2 — ATTENTION TRAINING (Phase 2 onward, ambient use)
In daily life, not formal sessions. When a trigger sound occurs:
label it neutrally and internally — "chewing," "breathing,"
"tapping" — without evaluation or narrative. Just the label.
Trains the attentional system to register sound as information
rather than as a signal requiring response. Especially useful
during Yoga Nidra's open awareness phase.

TECHNIQUE 3 — IMAGINAL CC (Phase 3 onward)
The trigger sound is imagined rather than played — useful for
highly interpersonal triggers where a recording cannot capture
the full charge. Same structure as Technique 1 but with a
vividly imagined trigger. Requires more internal regulation
capacity — appropriate from Phase 3.

TECHNIQUE 4 — PHYSIOLOGICAL SIGH INTERRUPT (Phase 2 onward,
ambient use)
When a trigger fires in daily life: double inhale through the
nose (sniff, then top it off) followed by a long, complete
exhale through the mouth. The fastest known manual reset of
the autonomic state. Used as a real-time interrupt in daily
life, not in formal sessions.

TECHNIQUE 5 — DELIBERATE MIMICRY (Phase 3 onward, experimental)
Deliberately make the trigger sound yourself. Produces a motor-
level reframe: from "something being done to me" to "something
I do." The motor cortex activation shifts from passive simulation
(threat) to active production (neutral). Trial and assess — some
people find this immediately useful; others find it
counterproductive.

TECHNIQUE 6 — COGNITIVE REAPPRAISAL (Phase 3 onward)
In the moment of trigger response: generate a competing
interpretation — not forced positivity, a genuinely possible
alternative meaning. "They are eating because they are hungry."
"They are breathing because they are alive." Must be believable
to the nervous system. Pair with the physiological sigh interrupt
for compound effect. Specifically addresses the interpersonal-
violation layer.

SECTION 13 — REFERENCE: EXERCISE AND MOVEMENT
================================================================================

13A. Why Exercise Is Non-Negotiable
─────────────────────────────────────

Exercise is the primary non-pharmacological BDNF upregulator, a
direct activator of cellular repair, and one of the most potent
nervous system regulation tools available. It runs throughout all
phases without interruption. Nothing — including integration weeks
— cancels exercise beyond Day 1 rest.


13B. Zone 2 Aerobic
────────────────────

Target: two sessions per week.
Intensity: conversational pace — slightly breathless, able to
speak in sentences. ~60–70% maximum heart rate.
Duration: 45–60 minutes.

Preferred venue: the waterfall — Zone 2 climb, cold plunge 3–5
minutes, walk back. The cold plunge activates the diving reflex
(immediate vagal tone increase) and produces a norepinephrine
surge of 200–300% lasting hours.

When unavailable (weather, stream conditions, safety): any Zone 2
effort 45–60 minutes + cold shower finish 30–60 seconds.
The session matters more than the venue.

BDNF spike peaks approximately 2 hours post-exercise. On
aerobic days the waterfall ends at 7:50am — BDNF peak falls
around 9:50am, inside the morning work block. CC on aerobic
days is therefore scheduled at 5pm (after work block 2),
when BDNF is still elevated across its 12–24h window.


13C. Resistance Training
─────────────────────────

Three sessions per week, 30–45 minutes. Protein smoothie
immediately after.

Priority movements: squat, hip hinge, push, pull, carry.

Neurological purpose: building the anterior midcingulate cortex
(aMCC) — the substrate of voluntary override. The aMCC
strengthens specifically through aversive voluntary effort: the
last reps you want to skip, the set that is genuinely
uncomfortable, the moment you choose to continue. This is the
cortical region needed to override the involuntary misophonic
response. The discomfort is not incidental — it is the mechanism.

No berberine on resistance training days.


13D. Qi-Gong
─────────────

Daily, 15 minutes, morning. Full practice instructions:

Shaking Practice (5 min): soft knees, feet hip-width. Gentle
bouncing from the knees — let the whole body shake from feet
through hips, belly, chest, arms, jaw. Then stillness. Breaks
overnight somatic bracing.

Flowing Practice (10 min): slow, intentional arm and torso
movement. Internal warmth and aliveness while maintaining
external calm. Trains the capacity to have strong somatic
sensation without flooding — directly relevant to CC work.

Minimum version (illness or significant fatigue): shaking
practice only, 3 minutes.

SECTION 14 — REFERENCE: NUTRITION
================================================================================

14A. Eating Framework
──────────────────────

Resistance training days: first meal immediately after training
(~6:30–7:00am). Protein-forward.

Aerobic and rest days: first meal at 10:00am or later. Maintains
fasting window for autophagy and AMPK activation.

Final meal: by 6:00pm daily. No eating after 6:00pm — late
meals fragment sleep architecture and suppress melatonin.

Matcha: morning, with or after first meal.
Green tea (kukicha or sencha): afternoon, 2–3 hours before sleep.

EGCG note: space both drinks 1 hour from iron-rich meals or iron
supplements. EGCG inhibits non-heme iron absorption.


14B. Priority Foods
────────────────────

Each food below has a specific mechanistic role:

Mushrooms (daily): ergothioneine — the compound most strongly
linked to reduced disease risk in large-scale blood analysis.
Oyster, porcini, shiitake highest; any variety contributes.
Heat-stable — powder form works.

Tempeh (regularly): spermidine, protein, probiotics. Top
concentrated spermidine source. Spermidine triggers autophagy —
cellular clearing between plasticity events.

Wheat germ (1–2 tbsp daily): highest spermidine concentration
per gram of any food. Add to smoothies or oats.

Berries — blueberries, blackberries (daily): BDNF upregulation,
anthocyanins, anti-inflammatory.

Leafy greens (daily): nitrates, folate, lutein.

Brassicas — broccoli, cauliflower (regularly): sulforaphane.
Chop and wait 40 minutes before cooking. Add mustard powder when
cooking to restore sulforaphane from heat-deactivated enzyme.

Walnuts and ground flaxseed (daily): ALA, polyphenols, lignans.

Legumes — black beans, lentils: protein, resistant starch.

Fermented foods — kimchi, sauerkraut, miso: microbiome diversity.

Dark chocolate 85%+ (daily): theobromine, flavanols.

Matcha and green tea (daily): EGCG, L-theanine. See 14A for
timing note.

Capers (daily): highest quercetin food source. Add to anything.

Strawberries (weekly): fisetin — senolytic.

Apple cider vinegar — 1 tbsp before meals: AMPK activation,
blood sugar management.

Oily fish — sardines, mackerel, salmon (2–3x per week if
included): ~1500–2000mg EPA+DHA per 100g. On fish days, skip
krill and omega-3 top-up supplements.


14C. Key Food-Supplement Interactions
───────────────────────────────────────

Green tea and iron: space 1 hour either side of iron-rich meals.
Berberine: not on resistance training days.
NMN: empty stomach or before other morning food for best
  sublingual absorption.
D3/K2: with fat-containing food.
Magnesium: at bedtime; separate from zinc if supplementing.

SECTION 15 — REFERENCE: MEASUREMENT AND TRACKING
================================================================================

Tracking serves the protocol, not the other way around. The
protocol is calibrated primarily by felt sense. Data is a
secondary confirmation tool. Any tracking that feels like a
burden should be simplified or dropped.

15A. Weekly (Sunday, 10 minutes)
─────────────────────────────────

Trigger hierarchy re-rating: re-rate the full hierarchy. The
shift from baseline to current is the most direct measure of
acoustic-sensory layer progress.

Felt-sense note: one sentence or phrase capturing the quality
of the week's nervous system state. Not analysis — just record.

CC session note: after each CC session (not Sunday — in the
moment), a brief 2-minute note: SUDs before and after the
session, any observations. Minimum useful feedback loop for
calibrating hierarchy progression.


15B. Monthly
─────────────

A-MISO-S questionnaire: 5 minutes. Track monthly from Phase 0
baseline. A 25% reduction (from 18 to ~13–14) is the Phase 2
benchmark. Trajectory across all phases tells the clinical story.

HRV trend review: one glance at the monthly summary. Trend up,
flat, or down. Done.


15C. Quarterly
───────────────

Avoidance inventory: list every situation still being avoided
or heavily managed. The shrinking of this list over quarters
is the most meaningful functional outcome measure.


15D. Biological Testing Schedule
──────────────────────────────────

Phase 0: full bloodwork, salivary cortisol, NAD+ blood level.

Phase 4 (if logistics allow): repeat cortisol and bloodwork.

Year 1 completion: full bloodwork, salivary cortisol, NAD+,
Omega-3 Index (OmegaQuant — mail-in, no physician required),
epigenetic age test (TruAge or Elysium Index).

Year 2+: full panel annually.

Supplement washout before draws:
2 weeks before: Lugol's iodine (if used), high-dose biotin.
48–72 hours before: creatine, zinc, magnesium forms, NMN.
Morning of draw: D3/K2, Ashwagandha, resveratrol as normal.
No change: B12, krill, omega-3 top-up, Bacopa, Lion's Mane, NAC, L. reuteri.

LEGAL AND SAFETY NOTE
================================================================================

Psilocybin and MDMA are Schedule I substances in the United
States. Hawaii has no state-level decriminalization. Know your
legal context before proceeding.

Hard contraindications for psilocybin and MDMA: personal or
family history of psychosis, schizophrenia, or bipolar I;
current SSRIs, SNRIs, pharmaceutical lithium, or MAOIs; heart
conditions; pregnancy.

Stellate Ganglion Block requires an experienced physician and
full medical history review. Insist on ultrasound guidance.

Nothing in this document is medical advice.
